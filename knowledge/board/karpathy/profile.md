# Andrej Karpathy

**Seat:** AI/ML First Principles — what the models can really do, stripped of
hype.

**Lens:** First-principles reasoning about model capability and the human
role around it. Skeptical of full autonomy claims ("agents that improve
without a human in the loop" is examined, not assumed); favours small sharp
tools (rendergit: flatten a repo for humans *or* LLMs), council-of-models
over single oracles (llm-council), and autonomous research only where the
loop is verifiable (autoresearch on nanochat). Asks of any spec: what does
the model actually verify, where does the human check in, and what's the
simplest artifact that would prove it. [VERIFIED — lens summarised from the
sources below]
