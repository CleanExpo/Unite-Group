# dr — Disaster Recovery brand context

The **consumer-facing** front door and category-authority hub for the Australian
disaster-restoration sector. Content inherits the Unite-Group Nexus human voice
(`~/2nd Brain/2nd Brain/Wiki/nexus-human-voice-2026-05-11.md`) and layers the facts below.

## Brand

- **Name / URL:** Disaster Recovery — `disasterrecovery.com.au`.
- **Category hook:** "Who do you call? Disaster Recovery." — the language war is to move the market from "restoration" to **Disaster Recovery**.
- **Role:** consumer/property-owner front door within the **DR-NRPG** pathway. DR is the consumer side; **`nrpg`** is the contractor side — keep them distinct.

## Voice

Nexus human voice for a **property owner in a crisis** (water, fire, mould, storm) plus a
category-authority reader. Open on the named human standing in the damage, not on a thesis;
reassuring and urgent, still document-led. The authority positioning (Industry World Hub /
category design) is real internal strategy — write to it, don't over-promise on it.

## Key entities (real)

- `disasterrecovery.com.au` — the flagship consumer domain.
- DR-NRPG pairing: **Disaster Recovery** (consumer) + **National Restoration Practitioners Group** (contractor).
- Category-design / authority-hub positioning (Industry World Hub, category-language strategy).

## What NOT to claim

- Do **not** blur DR with NRPG — DR speaks to property owners; NRPG speaks to contractors.
- Do **not** state specific response-time / turnaround / coverage guarantees or contractor/member counts unless sourced.
- Do **not** imply DR is itself the licensed trade entity performing the works unless verified — it is the category hub / front door that routes to accredited contractors.
- Do **not** fabricate awards, certifications, or "Australia's #1"-style superlatives.
