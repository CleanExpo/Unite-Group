## Digest
Australian construction and trade-facing procurement content should focus on practical planning rather than hard forecasts. The strongest CCW angle is that customers are making equipment, consumables, servicing, and cash-flow decisions under cost pressure. Content should help them reduce downtime, plan EOFY purchasing conversations with their accountant, and keep work moving with reliable machines and stocked essentials.

## Campaign hooks
- EOFY procurement certainty: lock service, stock, and maintenance plans before June pressure peaks.
- Supply-chain resilience: reduce downtime by planning consumables, machine servicing, and backup equipment early.
- Cost-pressure education: help customers compare whole-of-life reliability, not just lowest upfront machine price.

## Risk / compliance flags
- Do not claim guaranteed tax deductions, rebates, or savings. Use accountant-safe EOFY language only.
- Do not present market commentary as financial advice.
- Treat supply availability as stock-specific; avoid blanket availability promises.
- For CCW/Toby content, route through approval before public scheduling.
