# Duncan FITR Venture Brief

Purpose: capture the 2026-05-20 Plaud discussion on Duncan Perkins, FITR, the ITR Button, DIY Home Loan, and adjacent venture ideas without losing it inside raw transcript files.

## Core Venture Logic

The opportunity is an integrated finance, insurance, taxation, trust, estate, and retirement planning model: FITR. The strategic ambition is to make "FITR model" a category phrase in the same way "offset account" became generic market language.

The data and workflow center is not one product. It is a linked ecosystem:

- ITR Button / Dimitri: low-friction tax return intake, accountant referral, and lead generation.
- DIY Home Loan: rule-based debt recycling / investment-facility discipline with refund incentives.
- FITR concierge: coordinated finance, accounting, insurance, tax, trust, estate, and retirement planning.
- AI agents: enforce rules, gather documents, triage clients, generate recommendations, and maintain compliance evidence.

## ITR Button

Target buyers:

- accounting firms
- financial planners
- mortgage brokers
- employers with large workforces
- consumers who want cheaper or self-guided returns

Commercial shape:

- Free or low-cost consumer entry creates volume.
- Users can lodge directly or choose an accountant/referral partner.
- Accounting partners pay referral, subscription, or transaction fees.
- The Xero-style partner model is the reference: accountants become promoters because the platform helps their practice economics.
- The product should store minimum data needed: identity, contact, return timing, referral state, and enough audit metadata to support workflow; avoid holding unnecessary tax payloads.

Open design questions:

- exact referral-fee model
- unlock-key/payment flow
- MyGov/ATO/XPM boundaries
- accountant exclusivity and partner tiers
- compliance responsibilities under tax-agent and financial-advice rules

## DIY Home Loan

Recorded pricing concept:

- AUD 1,000/year fee.
- Fee is refundable if the client follows all 20 rules.
- Discount mechanic: every five followed rules can return AUD 250.
- Fee may be drawn from the line of credit and treated as part of the structure subject to professional advice.

Operating concept:

- Mortgage House or another lender provides facility support.
- Frollo/open-banking style data pulls bank data for budget and compliance checks.
- Payslips and other documents feed the AI agent at recurring review intervals.
- The AI agent tracks rule compliance and escalates when the client drifts.

## Market Context

The discussion treats Australian property/tax policy change as a catalyst. If investors move away from passive residential investment, demand increases for structured advice, cash-flow discipline, new-build strategies, debt recycling, trusts, estate planning, and tax-aware investment pathways.

This strengthens the case for FITR as a bundled advice-and-automation model, but it also raises regulatory exposure. Any product build must separate educational tools from licensed financial, credit, tax, and legal advice.

## Adjacent Venture Concepts

- Disaster Recovery funding: rapid make-safe cash-flow support for trades, potentially aligned with [[dr-nrpg]], Mortgage House, and insurer claim payments.
- Book Reviver: AI-assisted revival of stranded books into new digital, audio, and script assets with new rights/ISBN handling.
- Tea House: concierge transition service for seniors downsizing, coordinating real estate, finance, accounting, legal, and pension consequences.

## Required Gates

- Legal/regulatory map before product build.
- Data-minimisation and consent model.
- Partner agreement model for accountants, brokers, planners, and lenders.
- Unit economics model for transaction fees, referral fees, subscriptions, and facility fees.
- Prototype brief for [[unite-crm]] and [[synthex]] only after the compliance map is written.
