# ICP — Expanded NRPG Industry Association (Wave 0 Launch)

> **INTERNAL — Unite-Group portfolio only — do not distribute publicly 2026-05-11**

The seed `audience.primary` on `nrpg.ts` reads "industry training coordinators and response-network operators". That line is **too narrow** for the expanded association. The buyer for an ANZ-wide industry body is not the training coordinator; it is the firm owner-operator who signs the membership cheque, dispatches the crews, and carries the insurer relationships. This document overrides the BrandConfig seed and proposes a broader, sharper ICP that the channel-strategist, copywriter, and SEO-researcher will consume downstream.

---

## Primary ICP (named)

**ANZ restoration & specialty-cleaning contractor firm owner-operator** — a 5-50-employee independent business owner running an IICRC-aligned trade (water damage, fire/smoke, mould, storm response, carpet/hard-floor cleaning, IEP) across one to three Australian states (or NZ).

**Named archetype:** **Toby Bredhauer (Carpet Cleaners Warehouse / CCW)**. Owner-operator, national equipment distribution arm + service departments, current first paying SaaS client of Unite-Group, future co-founder/VP of the association itself. Toby is the canonical "if Toby would say yes to this email, the ICP would say yes". Every artifact downstream should be readable to Toby first.

Defining characteristics:
- Owns the business outright or with one partner. Decision is personal, not committee.
- Has crews in vans. Knows the insurer dispatch tree by first name.
- IICRC-certified at the individual level (WRT, FSRT, ASD, AMRT) but the firm is uncertified at the organisation level — no IICRC Certified Firm equivalent exists with ANZ teeth.
- Pays for software grudgingly. Pays for membership only if dispatch leads or insurer routing flow back through it.
- Already a member of zero, one, or many fragmented bodies (RIA/IICRC US, AIRAH, HIA, ISSA-via-distributor) and frustrated none of them do all of: certification, leads, advocacy, training, member-services-software in one bundle.

## Secondary ICPs

### Secondary 1 — Insurance claims handlers / loss adjusters at ANZ general insurers and IAG/Suncorp/QBE/Allianz panel firms

The supply-side of the dispatch surface. Their pain is *opposite-but-symmetric* to the contractor pain: they dispatch jobs to a panel of firms with wildly inconsistent quality, no shared certification floor, and no single point of accountability when a job goes sideways. An association that vouches for firm-level quality (Certified Firm tier) and offers a single advocacy counterparty is a relief, not a threat. **Wave-0 weight:** moderate — useful for the "Why this matters" backdrop in the founding-partners memo but **not** the launch-thread audience. Re-engage in Wave 2 (advocacy pillar).

### Secondary 2 — State building authorities and federal small-business policy contacts (Fair Trading NSW, Consumer Affairs Vic, QBCC, WA DMIRS; COSBOA member-association liaisons)

The legitimacy layer. They are not buying anything — they are being *recognised by* the association as counterparties for policy discussion. Wave-0 mentions them to anchor the "this is a real association, not a marketing stunt" framing. **Wave-0 weight:** low for launch comms, high for credibility footnotes. Re-engage in Wave 3 (COSBOA affiliation push).

**Of the two, Secondary 1 (insurance claims handlers) matters more for Wave-0 messaging** because the contractor-owner pain hierarchy is dominated by insurer-dispatch dynamics — and the founding-partners memo needs to gesture credibly toward that audience to land with the primary.

## Buyer Role + Power

- **Decision maker (signs the cheque):** The owner-operator. Solely. Membership decisions of <$5k/yr go through in one phone call. Of $5k-$25k/yr, one phone call + an internal "tell the bookkeeper" beat. Above $25k/yr (Certified Firm + full member-services bundle), 1-2 conversations with their spouse/business partner.
- **Champion (drives the project internally):** Often the owner is also the champion. Where the owner is older and operationally distant, the **operations manager** (sometimes called Ops Manager, GM, or "the 2IC") is champion. They feel the dispatch-chaos pain most acutely.
- **User (touches the tool / consumes the benefit):** Field technicians (use the training, carry the cert), the bookkeeper/admin (uses the member portal for billing/insurance docs), the ops manager (uses the dispatch surface + member software).
- **Blocker:** Accountant (only at very large firms) — challenges any subscription. Spouse/business partner. Existing certification body loyalists ("but the IICRC is the standard") — handled by the partner-not-fight positioning.
- **Influencer roles:** Head trainer (cares about CPD credit + LMS quality), regional manager (cares about cross-state reciprocity), preferred-supplier reps (e.g. CCW account managers — Toby's own salespeople carry the association message into every customer site).

## Pain Hierarchy (ranked top 5)

Ranked by frequency × severity × awareness. Awareness = "does the firm owner already articulate the pain in plain language, or only retroactively after it bites?"

### 1. Insurer dispatch routes leads randomly, often to firms that mishandle the scope and damage the relationship for everyone

- **Frequency:** Daily during storm season; weekly otherwise.
- **Severity:** Catastrophic — a botched job on a panel can lose the firm 6 months of insurer flow with one phone call.
- **Awareness:** Very high. Owners articulate this in the first 30 seconds of any conversation.
- **Plain-language version:** "The insurer's just chucking jobs at whoever picks up the phone. The bloke down the road did a mould job for IAG last month, did a hack job, and now IAG won't take my call." A trustworthy single dispatch surface backed by a Certified Firm tier solves this.

### 2. No ANZ-specific certification ladder that an insurer or regulator actually recognises

- **Frequency:** Weekly (every time a quote needs justification).
- **Severity:** High — caps the firm's premium pricing because every competitor claims the same "we're IICRC".
- **Awareness:** Medium-high. Owners know the cert problem exists; few have a fix.
- **Plain-language version:** "Anyone can put 'IICRC certified' on the van. There's no AU body checking, and the IICRC themselves are in the States — no skin in the game here." The 100-point NRPG cert + the new Certified Firm tier is the answer.

### 3. Marketing, branding, and tech burn weeks of owner time per quarter — and the agencies sold these services have all been duds

- **Frequency:** Monthly (a new agency pitch, a new tool subscription that doesn't get used).
- **Severity:** Medium-high — burns cash and the owner's attention without a measurable lift.
- **Awareness:** High but resigned. Owners articulate it as "the marketing crap" and have given up expecting it to work.
- **Plain-language version:** "Had three agencies in five years. Got a website out of one of them. The others ghosted after the second invoice." The Member-as-a-Service bundle (Synthex marketing-ops + brand-config + Unite tech) bypasses agencies entirely. This is the Member Services Pillar 1 moat in plain English.

### 4. No advocacy voice when insurers change scope rules or strip out line items mid-job

- **Frequency:** Quarterly (every policy review cycle).
- **Severity:** Catastrophic in aggregate — directly compresses margin.
- **Awareness:** Medium. Owners know they hate it; few connect the dots that an association could push back collectively.
- **Plain-language version:** "Insurer's chopped the deodoriser line item out of the scope template. Across 30 jobs that's $9k I can't bill anymore. Who do you even talk to about that?" RIA's AGA committee in the US is the template; an AU equivalent fills the gap. <!-- TODO verify exact AU deodoriser line-item scope-template history -->

### 5. Crew certification and CPD records live in 4 different bodies, 3 different PDFs, and the owner's email inbox

- **Frequency:** Daily (every time an insurer audit, or a job site asks "show me your tickets").
- **Severity:** Medium — usually annoying, occasionally job-stopping.
- **Awareness:** High. Owners articulate this as "paperwork hell".
- **Plain-language version:** "Mate, just need ASD on this job — can you find me the ticket?" / "Yeah it's in an email from 2023, hold on…". The CARSI LMS + association member portal solves the records-of-truth problem.

**Pain #6 (honourable mention) — equipment financing and supply.** Lower priority for Wave-0 messaging but covered through the CCW preferred-supplier integration.

## Trigger Events (what makes them buy THIS WEEK vs next year)

Specific moments that move a firm owner from "aware" to "actively shopping":

1. **A lost job to a competitor on the same insurer panel that handled it badly.** The phone call from the insurer's claims handler is the trigger. The owner finishes that call and Googles "ANZ restoration association" within the hour.
2. **Storm season warning issued by BoM.** Owners pre-position membership in cross-trade dispatch + advocacy ahead of forecast weather (Nov-Feb peak East Coast; Aug-Oct peak WA).
3. **Insurer scope-rule change letter arrives.** When QBE or IAG sends out a "we are updating the scope template for water damage effective 1 July" letter, the firm owner who reads it first becomes a member-services buyer within 14 days.
4. **A failed agency engagement just ended.** The agency invoice goes unpaid, the owner is mid-anger, and a member-services bundle priced at a fraction of an agency retainer lands at the right time.
5. **A peer / preferred-supplier (CCW account manager, IICRC instructor) name-drops the association.** Word-of-mouth from a trusted node beats any cold outreach. This is the lever Toby pulls.
6. **A regulator/Fair-Trading complaint hits the firm.** Suddenly the owner needs documented compliance, CPD records, and an advocacy phone number. Membership becomes a defensive purchase. <!-- TODO verify regulator-complaint-as-trigger frequency — anecdotal -->
7. **A John Coutis keynote at a related conference.** Coutis already speaks at corporate-resilience events; a contractor owner who happens to attend (or whose corporate insurance customer attends) hears the association referenced and follows up.

## Decision Process

Step-by-step from trigger to signed membership:

1. **Trigger event hits** (one of the 7 above).
2. **The owner Googles or asks a peer.** "ANZ restoration association" / "is the RIA in Australia". The first-page result that looks like a real organisation (not a one-page WordPress site) wins the click. SEO + LinkedIn presence matter here.
3. **Awareness scan — 24-72 hours.** Owner scans 3-5 candidates. They are testing for: (a) is this run by someone they know or trust? (b) does it actually deliver leads / advocacy / training? (c) is it AU-specific or another US import?
4. **Peer check.** Before the owner signs anything they ring 1-2 trusted peers and ask "have you heard of these blokes?" If the peer says "yeah, Toby's involved" or "saw John Coutis on their YouTube", the conversion probability triples.
5. **Trial-cost moment.** If membership is sub-$2k/yr and includes a tangible benefit (lead routing, certified-firm badge), the owner signs without escalation. If above $5k/yr the owner asks for a 30-day trial or a phone call with the founding partner.
6. **Signing the cheque.** Owner-operator signs personally. No procurement. No legal review except above $25k/yr.
7. **Post-purchase validation.** Within 30 days the owner is looking for: did a lead come through the platform? Did the cert show up in the member directory? Was the first member-services-bundle email actually useful or marketing fluff? If yes — they become an advocate. If no — they don't renew and they tell three peers.

**Average sales cycle:** 7-21 days for sub-$5k membership; 30-60 days for full Certified Firm + member-services bundle.

**Deal-breakers:**
- Looks like a US-only body with a "we have an AU page" afterthought.
- Membership benefits read as vague ("networking", "thought leadership") without concrete dispatch / leads / advocacy lines.
- No human face. Anonymous association = dead on arrival.
- Asking for a 12-month upfront annual fee with no monthly option. Owners cashflow-manage by the week.

## Coutis-Fit Assessment

**Verdict: Probably not directly known to the trade ICP. Awareness range: 10-25%. Useful as a halo asset, not as the primary credibility hook for the trade audience.**

Reasoning:

- John Coutis is well-known on the **AU corporate keynote and inspirational-speaker circuit** — large enterprise events, leadership offsites, resilience-themed conferences. <!-- TODO verify Coutis's actual keynote bookings 2024-2026 -->
- The ANZ restoration/cleaning trade ICP overlaps with the corporate-keynote audience only through (a) the larger firms (~50-employee tier) whose owners attend cross-industry SMB events, and (b) owners whose insurance customers are large corporates who've hosted Coutis. That's a thin overlap.
- The smaller-firm ICP (5-15 employees) — which is the majority of the addressable market — almost certainly has not encountered Coutis directly. Their media diet is industry-specific (R&R podcast, IICRC newsletters, INCLEAN, ServiceM8 webinars). Coutis's brand has not penetrated those channels.
- **Honest estimate:** ~10-15% of the primary ICP recognises the name on first sight; ~25-30% would recognise the face once shown a photo (because of LinkedIn algorithm bleed and corporate-event cross-pollination). Outright fans within the ICP: ~3-5%. <!-- TODO verify with primary research — 10-customer phone survey would resolve this in a week -->
- **Implication for Wave-0 messaging:** Lead with the *trade pain* + *Toby's involvement* + *the association's bundle*. Coutis is the *signal* that this association has reach beyond the trade — useful as a "this is bigger than a side project" credibility marker, NOT as the primary trust hook. The forbidden-angle "Coutis-as-inspirational-speaker framing" is doubly correct: even if the trade ICP knew him, that framing would alienate them. Coutis works as **the association's narrator and public voice**, anchoring video + podcast — let his presence accumulate authority over months, don't front-load it as the buying reason.

## Anti-ICP (who LOOKS like a fit but is wrong)

- **Sole-trader carpet cleaners (1-2 person, <$300k revenue).** Cannot afford member-services bundle. Will drag the brand into "another bottom-of-the-market association" territory. Re-engage in Year 2 with a stripped-down tier.
- **Large national franchises (Steamatic, Chem-Dry, Jim's Cleaning).** They have HQ-level decision-making, in-house training, and existing supplier relationships. Bureaucratic procurement. Not the Wave-0 buyer. May be a partnership conversation in Wave 3+.
- **Pure commercial cleaning contractors with no restoration scope.** Adjacent industry. Overlap exists but the dispatch / insurer / cert pain profile is materially different — they hire cleaners, not response crews.
- **US-based restoration firms with an AU office.** They already have RIA/IICRC membership at HQ. The AU office will defer up. Year-2 conversation, not Wave-0.
- **Homeowners and consumers.** Forbidden angle per brief. The association is B2B-trade only.
- **State-licensed plumbers and electricians who occasionally do emergency response.** Adjacent. The cross-trade dispatch story applies to them in Wave 2-3 but they're not the Wave-0 buyer.
