## Scope
Official documentation signals only (Google and Microsoft/Bing) relevant to:
1) structured data/schema and AI visibility,
2) AI answer/citation eligibility,
3) crawl/index/retrieval prerequisites.

## Google (official docs)

### 1) Structured data impact
- Google SEO starter guidance states structured data helps machines understand content and can enable rich results, but does **not** directly improve rankings.
- Direct quote surfaced by official guidance references: "structured data does not make your page rank better."

Primary references:
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/structured-data/search-gallery

### 2) AI features / AI Overviews eligibility
- Google AI features documentation points to Search technical requirements and policy compliance, not schema-only optimisation.
- AI-era guidance emphasises creating useful, people-first content and ensuring technical crawl/index accessibility.

Primary references:
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-features
- https://developers.google.com/search/blog/2025/05/succeeding-in-ai-search

### 3) Crawl/index prerequisites
- If Google cannot crawl/index content, it cannot appear in Search/AI surfaces.
- Core dependencies: crawl access, indexable responses, robots directives, Search Essentials compliance.

Primary references:
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/overview-google-crawlers
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/crawling-indexing/robots/intro
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/creating-helpful-content
- https://developers.google.com/search/docs/essentials

## Bing / Microsoft (official docs)

### 1) Structured data impact
- Bing guidelines position structured data as a way to help Bing understand pages and enhance presentation opportunities.
- No official claim found that schema alone guarantees ranking uplift or AI citation uplift.

Primary references:
- https://www.bing.com/webmasters/help/webmaster-guidelines-30fba23a

### 2) AI/Copilot inclusion controls
- Documented governance controls include robots.txt crawler directives, noindex/nocache handling, and removal workflows.
- Bing Webmaster stack adds URL submission, crawl controls, and diagnostics.

Primary references:
- https://www.bing.com/webmasters/help/content-removal-cb6c294d
- https://www.bing.com/webmasters/help/url-submission-62f2860b
- https://blogs.bing.com/webmaster/February-2026/Introducing-AI-Performance-in-Bing-Webmaster-Tools-Public-Preview
- https://blogs.bing.com/webmaster/June-2025/Start-Using-Bing-Webmaster-Tools-to-Improve-Your-Site-Visibility

### 3) Crawl/index prerequisites
- Bing requires crawlable, indexable, quality pages; indexing alone does not equal strong ranking/answer presence.
- Technical hygiene (robots, sitemap, submission, quality signals) remains foundational.

Primary references:
- https://www.bing.com/webmasters/help/webmaster-guidelines-30fba23a
- https://www.bing.com/webmasters/help/url-submission-62f2860b

## Translation into Synthex operating rules
1) Treat schema as **supporting infrastructure**, not a standalone growth lever.
2) Prioritise content usefulness, entity clarity, internal-link depth, and brand evidence over schema volume.
3) Enforce crawl/index health gates before AEO experimentation.
4) Measure citation/traffic/conversion lift after each change; roll back low-signal work.

## Evidence reliability note
This note is intentionally constrained to official vendor documentation links and avoids non-official SEO commentary as primary evidence.
