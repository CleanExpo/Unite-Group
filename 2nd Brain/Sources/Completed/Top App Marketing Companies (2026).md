## What is mobile app marketing?

Mobile app marketing means to make efforts so that your customers engage with your product and services on mobile. And it also means boosting your app’s presence in the app store with clever marketing techniques.

App marketing is all about getting your mobile application noticed, downloaded, and used by your target audience. It involves a mix of strategies designed to promote your app and ensure it stands out in a crowded marketplace. One of the key components is App Store Optimization (ASO), which is similar to SEO for websites.

Optimizing your app’s title, description, keywords, and visual elements like icons and screenshots will improve its visibility in app stores like Google Play and the Apple App Store. By making your app easy to find and appealing to potential users, you increase the chances of it being downloaded.

Once you’ve attracted users, the next step is to keep them engaged and encourage regular use of your app. This can be achieved through push notifications, in-app messaging, and loyalty programs that reward users for their continued engagement.

App marketing involves constantly analyzing and optimizing your strategies to ensure they are effective. So, using analytics tools to track user behavior, A/B testing different features and marketing messages, and gathering feedback all belong to app marketing.

## Stages of app marketing

While the app marketing funnel may include similar stages to the traditional digital marketing funnel, it consists of these three prime stages.

This concept is therefore all about interacting with your users through all three of these stages in order to drive profitable, long term action. So, the question now lies in how exactly do you interact with them to successfully promote your app.

Let’s take a closer look at the three phases.

### Awareness

The awareness stage occurs before marketers launch their apps, where it is all about creating a presence and building brand awareness. This strategy includes a clear brand purpose, communication and positioning – basically answering the who, when, why, where and how of your product and services.

The main goal in this stage is to interact with potential customers, by using methods, such as influencer marketing, and direct their attention to your upcoming brand.

### Acquisition

In the acquisition stage, your app has already launched and you are implementing new strategies to [continue to acquire users](https://www.businessofapps.com/marketplace/user-acquisition/) and generate installs.

Acquisition is an ongoing process that needs constant tweaking and extensions of previous strategies and methods of marketing.

So, the main focus here is to keep [optimizing for the app store](https://www.businessofapps.com/marketplace/app-store-optimization/) and implementing creative strategies to attract new users and continue to build a loyal customer base.

### Retention

When you acquire target users, it doesn’t end just there – you want to keep them engaged and invested in your app. In the retention stage, also an ongoing process, you want to come up with creative methods to turn users into loyal customers.

In this stage, make sure you keep up ongoing communication with users, maintaining an interactive and trustworthy bond.

In this case, [push notifications](https://www.businessofapps.com/marketplace/push-notifications/) will become your best friend. Keeping users in the loop and sending them alerts and updates about promotions, special rewards or upgrades is a great way to keep up engagement.

User experience is also an important component of the retention stage. First impressions are crucial – and you only get one chance at it. So make sure users can smoothly browse through your app, and use your services at ease from the start.

Mastering these three stages belong to a successful app marketing strategy. If you are able to build a presence for your upcoming app, acquire new users effectively and keep a loyal customer base, you are doing it all right.

But a lot of research and analysis goes into this, so it is easier said than done. This is why app marketing agencies and companies are here to make your life easier by lifting the extra weight off your shoulders.

### Influencer marketing

### Email marketing
