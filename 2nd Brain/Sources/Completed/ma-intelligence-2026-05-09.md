## Current ANZ SaaS Valuation Multiples (May 2026)

Source: Digital Asset Brokers Australia, SaaS Mag Consolidation Wave report.

- **Bootstrapped $500K–$1M ARR**: 4.0x–5.5x ARR
- **Bootstrapped $1M–$3M ARR**: 4.5x–6.5x ARR
- **Growth SaaS $1M–$5M ARR (>30% growth)**: 5.5x–8.0x ARR
- **Premium metrics (NRR >120%, Rule of 40 >50)**: 7x–10x+ ARR
- **Mature $10M+ ARR (EBITDA-positive)**: 10x–15x EBITDA
- **Local Australian buyers**: Apply 20–30% discount vs. international buyers (floor: 3.0x–4.5x)
- **Global PE benchmark (large-cap)**: 3.9x EV/Rev (vs. small-cap ANZ at 1.6x — sustained arbitrage gap)

**Key NRR thresholds driving premium/discount:**
- NRR <90%: Red flag, deal risk
- NRR 100–110%: Market-rate pricing
- NRR 120%+: Exceptional — triggers premium bid competition
- Monthly churn tolerance for PE entry: ≤2–3%
- CAC payback: <6 months = exceptional; <12 months = PE-fundable

## What Makes a $2B+ Target in 2026

Drawing from Altium ($8.8B, 20.7x), Domain ($3.0B, 25.2x), E2open ($3.4B), and the Qoria/RPM cluster ($1.0–$1.3B):

1. **ARR or Revenue scale**: Minimum $80M–$150M ARR to attract large-cap PE/strategic buyers
2. **Market position**: Category leader in a defensible vertical (not horizontal/commodity)
3. **AI defensibility**: Documented AI roadmap; buyers are walking from deals where AI can replicate the core product within 24 months
4. **NRR >115%**: Required for 8x+ multiples; signals product-market lock-in
5. **International revenue**: Inbound USD/GBP/EUR buyers pay a premium; AUD/USD exchange rate makes ANZ targets ~20–30% cheaper in real terms for US acquirers
6. **Rule of 40 >50**: Profitable or high-growth; PE operational playbook assumes AI-driven EBITDA expansion post-acquisition
7. **Clean IP + no technical debt**: Software M&A due diligence now includes AI disruption risk scoring (EY Australia framework)

## Implications for Unite Group ($2B by June 2028)
