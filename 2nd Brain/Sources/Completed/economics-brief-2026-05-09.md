# ANZ Business Climate & Economics Brief — 2026-05-09

## RBA Monetary Policy Decision — May 5, 2026

Source: RBA official media release mr-26-12; SBS live blog; CommBank economists; Canstar; Australian Property Experts.

### Decision

**Cash rate increased by 25 basis points to 4.35%** (from 4.10%)

- Board vote: **8-1** in favour of the increase
- Third consecutive rate hike in 2026
- Classified as a "widely expected" increase by all four major banks (ANZ, CBA, NAB, Westpac all predicted this move)

### RBA Board Statement — Key Language (Verbatim extracts)

- "Inflation picked up materially in the second half of 2025" with "capacity pressures" evident
- Middle East conflict has driven "sharply higher fuel and related commodity prices"
- "Early signs that many firms experiencing cost pressures are looking to increase prices"
- "Materially heightened uncertainties" regarding domestic activity and inflation
- "Plausible scenarios where inflation is higher and activity lower than envisaged"
- Board commits to being "attentive to the data and the evolving assessment of the outlook"

### Forward Guidance

- ANZ Bank economists: **No further cash rate hikes beyond May 2026** (ANZ call as of May 9)
- CommBank economists: "RBA has room to pause after May rate hike"
- Baseline forecast: Conflict resolution assumed → fuel prices decline → inflation peaks and falls as demand slows
- CPI context: March 2026 CPI at **4.6%** (per Australian Property Experts analysis triggering this decision)

## Impact of 4.35% Cash Rate on SaaS Multiples & M&A

### Direct Transmission Mechanism

Higher cash rates affect SaaS/tech M&A valuations through three channels:

1. **Discount rate compression**: Higher risk-free rate → higher WACC → lower DCF valuations → multiple compression. Rule of thumb: +100bps cash rate = approximately -0.5x to -1.0x ARR multiple compression for growth assets.

2. **Debt cost for PE buyouts**: Leveraged buyout economics deteriorate when debt costs rise. At 4.35% cash rate, floating-rate acquisition debt in Australia is pricing at ~7.5–8.5% all-in. PE sponsors require higher EBITDA margins or lower entry prices to maintain target IRRs (typically 20–25%).

3. **Buyer composition shift**: High rates favour **cash-rich strategic acquirers** (Canva, WiseTech Global, Atlassian) over PE sponsor buyouts. Strategic buyers can absorb rate pressure because they pay with equity/cash, not leveraged debt.

### Current Multiple Environment (Rate-Adjusted View)

The 4.35% cash rate is creating a visible spread between:
- **Strategic acquirers** (Canva, Caterpillar/RPM, WiseTech/E2open): Paying 8.6x–14.3x EV/Revenue — rate-insensitive because debt-free or minimal leverage
- **PE-backed buyouts** (Infomedia/TPG at 3.9x, Smartpay/Shift4 at 3.0x): Compressed multiples due to leveraged financing costs

**Practical implication for June 2028 exit**: A rate cut cycle (anticipated H2 2026 → 2027 if inflation resolves) would be a meaningful multiple expansion tailwind. Modelling a 3.85% cash rate by mid-2027 (Canstar base case) implies +0.5x–1.0x ARR multiple recovery for growth SaaS assets.

## ANZ Tech Sector Investment Climate — May 2026

Source: PwC Australia M&A Outlook 2026; ION Analytics Dealspeak APAC; William Buck Dealmaking Insights 2026.

### Macro Environment

- **Australia M&A hit a 4-year high in 2025** (ION Analytics/Mergermarket data) — momentum carrying into H1 2026
- **Foreign investors acquired 45% of deal value** in Australian M&A in 2025 (PwC data)
- **Local CEO acquisition intent**: 68% of Australian CEOs surveyed by PwC plan an acquisition in 2026 — highest since 2021
- **Inbound interest**: US and UK PE/strategics remain active; AUD/USD rate makes ANZ targets 20–30% cheaper in real terms for USD buyers

### Tech Sector Specifics

- **AI-adjacent assets command premium**: "Nearly half of all tech deals now have an AI component" — non-AI software faces valuation discount
- **Take-private opportunity persists**: ANZ small-cap software (~$50M–$200M market cap range) trades at 1.6x revenue vs. 3.9x large-cap — 2.3x gap = sustained PE arbitrage
- **Due diligence evolution**: EY Australia and PwC both flagging "AI disruption risk scoring" as a new standard in software M&A due diligence — buyers want to know if the product can be replicated by AI within 24 months
- **Vertical SaaS premium**: 46% of 2025 SaaS M&A was vertical SaaS; category-leader verticals commanding 8x–14x multiples vs. 3x–4x for horizontal

### Financing Conditions

- **PE dry powder**: Substantial — Australian PE had record committed capital as of March 2026 (William Buck report)
- **Deal financing**: Improved vs. 2023/2024 — lenders returned to tech M&A at 4.35% base rate, though PE IRR math is tighter than 2021/2022 era
- **Government tailwinds**: Australian Federal Budget 2026 included $A2.4B in tech/AI investment stimulus — supporting sector confidence

## June 2028 Exit Window: Macro Scenario Analysis

The $2B by June 2028 target depends on macro conditions at exit. Three scenarios:

### Scenario A — Base Case (Probability: 55%)
- RBA cuts to ~3.50%–3.75% by mid-2027 as inflation resolves
- AUD/USD stabilises at ~0.68–0.72
- Tech M&A multiples recover +0.5x–1.0x ARR from current levels
- Strategic buyer pool: WiseTech, Canva, global PE (Thoma Bravo, Vista) all active
- **Implication**: Exit window opens cleanly in H1 2028 with multiple expansion tailwind

### Scenario B — Inflationary Persistence (Probability: 30%)
- RBA holds at 4.35% or hikes once more to 4.60% by H2 2026
- Multiple compression continues; PE buyout math stays tight
- Strategic acquirers remain the primary buyer pool (higher cash/equity, rate-insensitive)
- **Implication**: Exit still achievable at $2B+ but requires ARR scale to compensate for multiple compression — NRR >120% and $150M+ ARR needed

### Scenario C — Recession / Global Shock (Probability: 15%)
- Middle East escalation drives sustained fuel/commodity inflation
- RBA forced to cut rates aggressively to support economy (paradoxically good for asset prices)
- Global M&A pauses for 6–12 months then surges on PE dry powder deployment
- **Implication**: June 2028 window could shift to H2 2028 / H1 2029; fundamentals of the business matter more than macro timing
