Marketing isn’t just about selling products – it’s about telling stories that resonate with people. The most successful marketing campaigns of all time have done just that: captured attention, sparked conversation, and even changed societal norms.

From iconic campaigns like Nike’s *Just Do It* to more recent digital innovations by brands like Mattel and Netflix, the best marketing campaigns are not just memorable but impact culture, consumer behaviour, and social issues.

Let’s explore some of the best and most powerful marketing campaigns ever made, and the reasons behind their success.

## Timeless classics and the marketing campaigns that set the standard

### Nike – Just Do It (1988)

Nike’s *Just Do It* campaign is a true marketing legend. Launched in 1988, it became a global mantra for empowerment and ambition. The campaign featured both professional and amateur athletes, highlighting personal stories of achievement. The tagline resonated deeply with a generation focused on fitness and self-expression. Today, it still echoes Nike’s commitment to social justice initiatives, aligning the brand with movements like Black Lives Matter, which has only strengthened its global appeal.

[Source: Medium](https://medium.com/@ncreus.ieu2021/echoing-beyond-boundaries-the-timeless-impact-of-nikes-just-do-it-fb62207147de)

### Volkswagen – Think Small (1960s)

In a time when bigger was considered better, Volkswagen’s *Think Small* campaign broke the mould by embracing clever minimalism. It changed perceptions about small cars in a post-war world, using humour and simplicity to stand out in a crowded market. The campaign’s impact on advertising was profound, showing that wit and a fresh approach could redefine industry standards.

[Source: Financial Times](https://www.ft.com/content/2569dc0e-679d-11df-a932-00144feab49a)

### Apple – 1984 Super Bowl Commercial

Apple’s *1984* Super Bowl commercial was more than an ad; it was a statement. It positioned Apple as a tech rebel, challenging the conformity of the corporate world through cinematic storytelling. It tapped into societal fears of a technological dystopia, aligning Apple with creativity, individuality, and freedom. This campaign’s influence can still be seen today in Apple’s branding strategy and marketing tactics.

![Apple 1984 Super Bowl Commercial](https://welovesalt.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/insights-market-insights-best-marketing-campaigns-apple-1200x647px.webp)

### De Beers – A Diamond is Forever (1999)

De Beers revolutionised the diamond industry with the *A Diamond is Forever* campaign, making diamond rings the ultimate symbol of love and commitment. By positioning diamonds as essential to romantic milestones, De Beers created a cultural norm that still influences consumer behaviour today.

[Source: The Eye of Jewelry](https://theeyeofjewelry.com/de-beers/de-beers-jewelry/de-beers-most-famous-ad-campaign-marked-the-entire-diamond-industry)

### Coca-Cola – Share a Coke (2011)

The *Share a Coke* campaign was a brilliant move in personalisation. Coca-Cola replaced its brand name with popular first names on bottles, encouraging consumers to find a Coke with their name or the name of a friend. The campaign resonated deeply with younger audiences on social media, sparking viral sharing. It didn’t just drive sales; it created an emotional connection between the brand and its customers.

[Source: Medium](https://medium.com/@akshathas721/share-a-coke-through-the-years-431f51ea6fa8)

## Recent disruptive marketing campaigns (2023–2024)

### Barbie – Barbiecore (2023)

Mattel’s *Barbiecore* campaign has been dubbed the marketing event of the century. The movie tie-ins, social media trends, and strategic influencer partnerships created a cultural phenomenon. The campaign’s success lies in its ability to tap into both nostalgia and modern empowerment, appealing to a diverse audience through vibrant aesthetics and inclusive messaging. Barbie not only revived a beloved brand but also sparked global conversations about femininity and identity.

## Marketing campaigns driving social and environmental change

### ALS Ice Bucket Challenge (2014)

The ALS Ice Bucket Challenge is one of the first viral campaigns to use social media for activism. The campaign raised millions for ALS research and brought awareness to the disease in an innovative, participatory way. It’s a textbook example of how marketing can turn a cause into a cultural moment, leveraging the power of social media to inspire action.

![People taking part in the ALS Ice Bucket Challenge ](https://welovesalt.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/insights-market-insights-best-marketing-campaigns-als-ice-bucket-challenge-1200x647px.webp)

[Source: The Independent](https://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/home-news/report-pours-cold-water-on-ice-bucket-charity-funding-9953210.html)

### Corona – Pay with Plastic (2019)

Corona’s *Pay With Plastic* campaign tackled environmental issues head-on by encouraging consumers to exchange plastic waste for limited-edition beer. The initiative, in partnership with Parley for the Oceans, raised awareness about plastic pollution and created a direct link between consumer action and environmental responsibility.

![Woman engaging in Corona Pay with Plastic campaign](https://welovesalt.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/insights-market-insights-best-marketing-campaigns-corona-1200x647px.webp)

[Source: Businesswire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20190604006171/en/Corona-Swaps-Sales-Plastic-World-Oceans-Week)

### Always – Like a Girl (2014)

Always’ *Like a Girl* campaign was a game-changer for gender representation in advertising. It challenged the negative connotations of the phrase “like a girl” and turned it into a symbol of empowerment. The campaign sparked global conversations about gender equality and reinforced Always’ commitment to female empowerment.

[Source: Beloved Brands](https://beloved-brands.com/always-like-a-girl/)

## What makes these marketing campaigns stand out?

These campaigns stand out because they inspire, challenge, and resonate with their audiences on a deeper level.

Take Nike’s *Just Do It* for example. This campaign didn’t just sell sneakers; it became a global rallying cry for motivation and empowerment. It connected with people on a personal level, turning Nike into a brand that stands for ambition and perseverance.

Similarly, Always’ *Like a Girl* campaign turned a phrase typically used as an insult into a symbol of empowerment. By challenging societal stereotypes, Always sparked global conversations about gender equality, helping to redefine what it means to do something “like a girl.”

What makes these campaigns stand out is their ability to:

- **Create emotional connections:** Nike’s empowerment message and Always’ celebration of strength tap into universal emotions.
- **Challenge societal norms:** Both campaigns reshaped how consumers perceive fitness and gender, using marketing to spark social change.
- **Inspire long-term loyalty:** These campaigns aren’t just about selling—they create lasting relationships with consumers by aligning with values that matter.

### Broader trends in marketing and society

The best international marketing campaigns today reflect broader societal and marketing trends. They are about much more than products. hey’re about building communities, creating meaningful connections, and reflecting consumer values.

- **Inclusivity and empowerment:** Dove’s *Real Beauty* campaign is a perfect example of how brands are using marketing to drive change. It challenges traditional beauty standards, promoting diversity and self-confidence. Consumers want brands to stand for something, and Dove answered that call.
- **Sustainability:** Brands like Patagonia and Corona are leading the way with campaigns that integrate sustainability into their messaging. *Pay With Plastic* encouraged consumers to help clean up beaches, making environmental action a part of the consumer experience.
- **Digital transformation:** Duolingo and Netflix show how brands can leverage digital platforms to build authentic, relatable relationships with their audience. Duolingo’s use of humour and its viral mascot strategy have made it a household name, particularly among younger audiences.

These demonstrate that the best marketing campaigns of all time are rooted in societal change, digital innovation, and a deep understanding of consumer values.

### Doing more with marketing

As we have seen, the best marketing campaign have the power to influence cultural norms, drive social change, and tackle global challenges. These campaigns promote empower and drive digital innovation, making marketing a force for change that shapes how we view the world and each other.
