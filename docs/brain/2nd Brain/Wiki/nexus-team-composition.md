# Unite-Group Nexus — World-Class Agency Team Composition

Researched from: AKQA, Publicis Sapient, R/GA, McKinsey Digital, Stripe, Linear, Vercel, Anthropic (May 2026).

## Current Agent Roster

## The 10 Missing Roles

### Tier 1 — Quality Gates (build first)

**QA Lead Agent**
- Holds every client deliverable against a pass/fail rubric before it ships
- Without this, scaling makes errors systemic — one hallucination destroys retention
- Source: Publicis Sapient runs Quality Engineering as a full specialist track

**Brand Guardian / Editorial Standards Agent**
- Quality gate for all non-code output: copy, reports, content, social posts
- Enforces brand voice and factual accuracy before anything enters client portal
- Source: Top-tier agencies build this into delivery process at brief stage

### Tier 2 — Connective Tissue (the roles mid-market skips)

**Solutions Architect Agent**
- Designs end-to-end system architecture before engineers write a line
- Currently: IDD builders receive briefs directly from the Board → rework risk
- Source: Every Publicis Sapient and McKinsey Digital engagement leads with this role

**Creative Strategist Agent**
- Translates Board directives into tight, performance-grounded creative briefs
- Currently: Brand Genius works from broad instructions → quality variance
- Source: AKQA and W+K treat this as a primary discipline separate from creative

**CX Strategist Agent**
- Maps the full client customer journey, identifies highest-leverage interventions
- [[ccw]]'s trade customer journey is currently unmapped
- Source: AKQA runs Senior CX Strategist as a standalone global role in APAC

### Tier 3 — Scale Output Quality

**Traffic Manager / Resource Orchestrator Agent**
- Manages agent utilisation, prevents context collision across parallel workstreams
- Critical when roster exceeds 40 agents
- Source: Top agencies have dedicated headcount; mid-market uses spreadsheets

**Design Engineer Agent**
- Spans Figma to production React — closes the design-to-code quality gap
- Source: Vercel built an entire Design Engineering team around this principle

**AgentOps Agent**
- Owns production reliability, cost, latency, and security of the agent fleet
- As paying clients depend on the system, this becomes a commercial obligation
- Source: Anthropic, Stripe list this as a specialist emerging role

### Tier 4 — Revenue Acceleration

**GTM Engineer Agent**
- Builds automated client acquisition: personalised outreach, lead scoring, CRM automation
- Source: Vercel lists GTM Engineer as a specialist role

**Forward-Deployed Agent**
- Embeds with a client ([[ccw]] first), observes friction, brings back product intelligence
- Source: Stripe lists Forward Deployed Engineer in Professional Services

## The Elite Principle (most important insight)

> The connective tissue roles are what mid-market agencies skip. CX Strategist, Solutions Architect, Creative Strategist — none generate billable output directly, but all protect the quality of the output that does. Elite agencies fund them. Mid-market agencies call them overhead.

**Design engineering is the quality multiplier.** One person who can design and ship production code eliminates quality decay between concept and live product. This is the highest-leverage hire at Vercel and the highest-leverage agent to build at Unite-Group.

**QA is a revenue protection role, not an overhead.** Every top-tier agency treats quality assurance as a delivery gate. One bad client deliverable undoes months of retention work.

## Build Priority

1. QA Lead + Brand Guardian (before scaling any client work)
2. Solutions Architect (before any new client build)
3. Creative Strategist + CX Strategist (for [[ccw]] sprint 3+)
4. Traffic Manager + Design Engineer + AgentOps (at 40+ agents)
5. GTM Engineer + Forward-Deployed (post-[[ccw]] validation)
