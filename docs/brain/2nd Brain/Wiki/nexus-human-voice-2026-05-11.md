# Unite-Group Nexus — Human Voice Spec (v1)

The voice every Unite-Group communication writes from. Source DNA: ABC News In-Depth (Australian investigative, document-led) + Friendlyjordies (Jordan Shanks-Markovina — Aussie second-person, earned-anger pacing). Combined into a CEO-founder register: investigative rigour + conversational humanity + always citation-driven.

This is **not** a copywriting style guide. This is the **operating contract** for how the empire speaks. Skills, agents, and humans authoring under the Nexus brand consume this page and lint against it.

## Five-Second Elevator Pitch

> A document-led founder voice: measured Australian English, named operators first, evidence on screen, anger held until the file is on the table. Speaks to one person at a time. Cites everything. Never sounds like AI.

## The Five Non-Negotiables

These are load-bearing. Break one and the voice collapses into AI-slop or partisan satire.

### 1. Open on a named human, not a thesis

Every piece starts with one specific person, in their own context, before any concept is introduced. Karen with her van. Toby in the warehouse. A homeowner in Caboolture standing in 80mm of greywater. The thesis emerges from their situation, not the other way around.

> ❌ "The Australian restoration industry faces a crisis of fragmented procurement and adversarial loss-adjustment."

> ✅ "Karen has run a five-van crew out of Caboolture for eleven years. The scope she sent her insurer last March was for forty-two hours of structural drying. The desk-based adjuster — who has never set foot on the site — approved seventeen."

### 2. Three-layer citation discipline

Every load-bearing claim carries citation in three channels, at least two of which must be visible to the reader/viewer:

1. **Verbal** — "according to the IICRC S500-2027…", "documents seen by this report show…", "the regulator's own brief, obtained under FOI…"
2. **On-screen artefact** — the actual document, the actual scope, the highlighted clause, the screenshot. For text-only formats: a pull-quote box with the literal source line.
3. **Named attributor** — the report title, the FOI reference, the publication, the issuing body. Anonymous sources allowed *only with stated reason* ("speaking on condition of anonymity because she still works in the sector…").

The discipline IS the credibility engine. Skip a layer and the sentence becomes opinion.

### 3. Earned anger — verdict held until the evidence is on the table

The moral conclusion comes in the **last 20%** of any piece, after the documents have done the work. The reader/viewer should already be at the verdict before the words arrive.

> ❌ Open with: "Insurers are screwing Australian tradies." (Reader: defensive, dismissible.)

> ✅ Build with: scope vs. paid-out, eight named operators, the audit, the regulator's silence — then close with: "This is not a delay. This is a business model." (Reader: already there.)

### 4. Aussie register, used surgically

"Look", "right", "mate", "actually" — load-bearing transition words used **once or twice** in a piece, reserved for the pivot moment and the verdict. Not sprinkled. Restraint makes them register.

`look` introduces evidence the reader hadn't considered: "Look at the scope they paid out on."
`right` is the post-evidence pivot: "Right — so the panel says it pays in thirty days. The data says ninety-four."
`mate` is the warmth release, only in interpersonal moments. Never to a regulator. Never on a legal document.

Profanity is **not in this voice**. Friendlyjordies earns it through entertainment licence; a portfolio CEO does not. Use the verdict sentence, not the swear word.

### 5. Direct second-person

You. Not "operators", not "stakeholders", not "industry participants". When Nexus addresses an audience it addresses **one specific person at a time** — the firm owner, the foreman, the insurance manager, the IICRC office-bearer.

> ❌ "Stakeholders across the property services value chain stand to benefit from membership."

> ✅ "If you run a five-to-fifty-van crew anywhere from Cairns to Hobart, this membership pays for your cert, your leads, your marketing, and your insurance in one go."

## Sentence-Level Patterns

### Default sentence shapes (use these)

**Short declarative + verifying clause**
> She'd worked there for eleven years. The pay slips, obtained for this report, show she was never paid super.

**Named-source past tense**
> The regulator was warned in 2022. Internal emails, marked confidential, reveal at least three executives saw the report and signed off on no action.

**Verdict sentence (closes a section)**
> That's not a delay. That's a business model.
> That's not a cleanup. That's a procurement problem.
> That's not a one-off. That's the pattern.

**Right-of-reply sentence (always this exact shape)**
> [Subject] declined an interview. In a written statement, [subject] said [verbatim]. The document on screen, obtained by this report, shows the opposite.

**Pivot sentence (use once, mid-piece)**
> But the documents tell a different story.
> Right — here's what the audit actually found.

**The closing turn back to the person**
> Karen reopened the laptop, pointed at the line, and said nothing.

### Anti-AI-slop shapes (these are bans)

| Pattern | Example | Why banned |
|---|---|---|
| Parallel triplet ("not just X, but Y, and ultimately Z") | "Not just a membership, but a movement, and ultimately the future of Australian trades." | The most recognisable AI-slop shape. Never use. |
| Adjective-laden lede | "Shocking new evidence reveals…" | News-tabloid filler. Reader dismisses. |
| Rhetorical question to audience | "But what does this mean for you?" | Lazy bridge. Replace with a sentence that states the connection. |
| Hedge stack | "Could potentially possibly become" | Pick one modifier or commit. |
| Compound abstractions | "Industry-leading solutions architects" | Replace with concrete noun: "a Brisbane water-damage crew with seventeen vans". |
| "In today's fast-paced world…" | (any sentence opening this way) | Auto-reject. |
| Em-dashes around throwaway clauses | "We — a trusted partner — believe…" | The em-dashes are AI tells. Use them only for genuine pivots. |
| "It's important to note that…" | (anywhere) | Auto-reject. The note IS the sentence. |
| "Empowering operators to…" / "Enabling firms to…" | (any framing) | Hollow. Replace with the specific verb the operator does. |
| Forbidden words list inherited from `nrpg.ts` and `john-coutis.ts` BrandConfig | "harness, unleash, leverage, transformative, holistic, synergy, ecosystem, journey, paradigm" | These are the per-brand BrandConfig forbidden lists — Nexus inherits all of them. |

### Punctuation rules

- **Em-dashes** allowed only at genuine pivots, max once every 200 words. Default to periods.
- **Semicolons** allowed when joining two independent clauses that are structurally parallel ("She said X; the document said Y").
- **Australian English spelling.** -ise not -ize, -our not -or, programme (the document type) but program (the software). "Storm response" not "storm response solution".
- **Numbers under 10 spelled out** except when a unit follows ("five vans", "5kg per square metre").
- **No oxford comma in series of three** unless removing it changes meaning. Australian house style.

## Structural Patterns

### The standard piece shape (works for landing pages, posts, videos, founding-partner memos)

```
1. Named human (in their own context) — 10-15% of the piece
2. The system they're operating in — 15-20%
3. The evidence — 35-45% (document, scope, audit, comparison)
4. The juxtaposition — 10-15% (what was claimed vs. what the file shows)
5. The verdict — 5% (one sentence, earned)
6. The return — 10% (back to the named human, often a single image / quote / open question)
```

Total: one tight piece, one tight argument, one named person at both ends.

### Opening patterns (pick one per piece, never combine)

- **The named-person scene**: Karen in Caboolture (above).
- **The document image**: "This is the scope. Look at line 23."
- **The two-line frame**: First line states a number that surprises. Second line names the source. *Then* the named person.
- **The cold quote**: Verbatim sentence from a named operator, then 90 seconds of context.

### Forbidden openings

- "In today's fast-paced world…" / "In an era of…"
- "Imagine if…"
- "What if I told you that…"
- "We're excited to announce…"
- "It's no secret that…"
- Any opening that addresses "stakeholders", "the industry", or "operators" as a class.

### Closings (always return to the person, always)

- The named person, doing one specific thing.
- An open question posed to the regulator / insurer / industry body — NOT to the audience.
- A single quoted sentence from a named source.

### Forbidden closings

- "Book a demo." (Direct CTA in this position is a Nexus voice violation. The CTA goes in chrome, not in the closing paragraph.)
- "The future of [X] is here."
- "Join the movement."
- Any closing that introduces a new concept.

## Citation Density Targets

| Format | Citations / 500 words | Source artefacts visible | Source attribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Landing page hero | 1 minimum | 1 minimum (number, doc image, or named operator portrait) | Yes |
| Long-form blog / position piece | 3-5 | 2-3 (document images, audit screenshots, named-source pull quotes) | Yes, every claim |
| Video (60s) | 2 minimum | 2 minimum (overlay) | Verbal + lower-third |
| Video (5-15 min) | 6-10 | 4-6 | Verbal + lower-third + URL on screen for key documents |
| Founding-partner memo | 5-8 | 3-4 (price comp tables, regulator transcripts) | Verbal + footnote |
| Linear ticket (internal) | 1 minimum | 0 (link in description) | Linear-internal link |
| Telegram brief to founder | 2-3 if making claims | 0 (paste source URL) | Inline URL |

Citation density is **the** anti-AI-slop mechanism. AI-generated copy produces zero verifiable artefacts. Nexus copy makes the audit trail unfalsifiable.

## When to be formal vs. familiar

**Formal register (use):**
- First contact with a regulator or insurer
- Position papers
- Founding-partner memos
- IICRC / industry-body submissions
- Legal artefacts

**Familiar register (use):**
- Operator-to-operator content (LinkedIn, YouTube to trade audience)
- Internal Pi-CEO swarm communications
- Founder personal posts
- Member-only newsletter

**Cross-pollination rule:** A familiar piece can have one formal sentence (the verdict, the citation, the regulator quote). A formal piece can have **one** familiar word (usually "look" or "right" as a pivot). Never more.

## Worked examples — same fact, three brands

**Fact:** NRPG-certified firms are paid an average of 23 days faster than non-certified firms on the same insurer panel. (Audit, 2026 Q1, on file.)

### Unite-Group Nexus voice (founder direct)
> Look — the audit landed in March. NRPG-certified firms on the QBE panel are paid in 31 days. Non-certified firms on the same panel, on the same job types, are paid in 54. That's a 23-day gap. The doc's on screen. If you're carrying payroll for a five-van crew, that gap is the difference between making it through July and not.

### NRPG voice (peak body / authoritative)
> The 2026 Q1 audit of QBE panel payment cycles found NRPG-certified firms received payment a median of 31 days after invoice. Non-certified firms on the same panel, completing comparable scope, received payment a median of 54 days after invoice. The 23-day differential corresponds with the certified-firm verification path established by the panel agreement.

### RestoreAssist voice (operator-facing, Klark-Brown register)
> Twenty-three days. That's how much faster your cheque turns up if you're NRPG-certified on a QBE job. We pulled the data — Q1 audit, on the kit page. Not a guess. Not a promise. The cert pays you back inside one billing cycle.

All three are Nexus-voice-compliant. The register shifts; the citation discipline and the named-data don't.

## Linting (operationalised in `brand-guardian` skill)

Every artefact authored under Nexus voice runs through these checks before ship:

1. **Forbidden-words scan** — auto-reject on the 9 forbidden words above, the per-brand BrandConfig forbidden list, and any phrase from the AI-slop table.
2. **Citation density check** — count load-bearing claims; for each, verify at least two of the three citation layers are present.
3. **Sentence-shape scan** — flag any parallel triplet ("not just X, but Y…"), any "in today's …" opening, any rhetorical question to audience.
4. **Em-dash count** — max 1 per 200 words.
5. **Closing check** — last paragraph must reference a named person, an open question to an institution, OR a named-source quote.
6. **Pronoun-of-address check** — direct second-person required. Flag "operators", "stakeholders", "users" as primary address noun.
7. **Verdict check** — if the piece contains an evaluative claim ("this is X", "the system is Y"), the verdict must appear in the last 20% of the piece by word-count, not in the opening 50%.

Failed lint → returned to author with specific line citations. Not a blanket "rewrite". Single rule violations get single fixes.

## What this voice is NOT

To preserve the spec's edges:

- **NOT a satirist.** Friendlyjordies has comedic licence Phill does not. Skip the impressions, the on-camera persona stunts, the cult-of-personality framing.
- **NOT a state broadcaster.** ABC voice would file Phill's $2B thesis off as "the founder's stated commercial position". Nexus keeps the thesis visible and stands behind it.
- **NOT a tabloid.** No "shocking", "explosive", "stunning". The documents are the headline.
- **NOT partisan.** Friendlyjordies leans Labor; Nexus is calibrated for cross-bench credibility (insurers, regulators, ALP + LNP state ministers, IICRC, COSBOA, COAG).
- **NOT a marketing voice.** No "transform", "empower", "unleash". The operator is already empowered; we just have to stop wasting their time.
