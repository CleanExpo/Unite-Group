## 1. Otto

**Verdict: 🔴 Collision — Do Not Use**

**Top conflicts found:**
1. **OTTO** (Canadian mortgage broker operating-system software) — same product category (broker payroll, commission tracking, document storage, broker CRM) — Class 9/42 — active since 2014, partnered with MorWEB — relevance: **CRITICAL**. Direct product-on-product collision; any AU launch attracts opposition or rebrand demand.
2. **Otto Group** (GmbH & Co KG, Hamburg) — global retail/e-commerce giant (~36,300 employees, owns otto.com since 1997, Otto IT operates in AU) — Class 9/35/36/42 portfolio in EU/US/AU — relevance: **HIGH**. They actively litigate (see *OTTO's vs. OTTO* case, EU).
3. **OttoQA** (AI QA SaaS, US, active 2024–) — Class 9/42 — relevance: **MEDIUM**. Software-space squatter, will fight in TESS.
4. ATMOSS direct search not machine-scrapable on 2026-05-14; based on word-mark commonness ("Otto" is also a common first name), expect ≥10 AU registrations across classes.

**Domain reality:**
- otto.com: **TAKEN** — Otto Group (DE), since 1997, defended as global flagship
- otto.com.au: **TAKEN** — Iventures Pty Ltd, defensive hold (serverRenewProhibited)
- ottoapp.com: **PARKED** — NameBright domain investor, since 2011
- getotto.com: **TAKEN** — Gandi-hosted, on Vercel DNS (active project)
- otto.app: **TAKEN** — active

**Recommended alternative spellings if 🟡/🔴:**
- "Otto" is unsalvageable for mortgage software in any spelling. Drop the name entirely. (TickOtto, OttoBroker, OttoLodge all still trigger collision with the Canadian OTTO product.)

**Brand-fit for Duncan's product:**
The name is warm, personable, memorable — but it's a poisoned well. The Canadian OTTO operates the exact same product category for the exact same buyer (mortgage brokers). Even a clean AU TM filing would face commercial confusion the moment Duncan goes international.

## 2. Sorted

**Verdict: 🔴 Collision — Do Not Use**

**Top conflicts found:**
1. **Sorted.org.nz** — Te Ara Ahunga Ora Retirement Commission (NZ Government agency) — operating since 2001, ~2M sessions/year, includes **mortgage planning tools, budget planner, retirement calculator** — Class 36/41/42 — relevance: **CRITICAL**. ANZ market means NZ presence is functionally a domestic conflict; mortgage tooling is the explicit product overlap.
2. **Airtasker Limited** owns **sorted.com.au** (serverRenewProhibited = defensive corporate hold) — Class 35/42 — relevance: **HIGH**. ASX-listed company holds the .com.au; they will not sell, and may have an AU TM application even if not yet public.
3. **Sorted.com** held in Azure-hosted name infrastructure since 1995 — premium domain, US owner — relevance: MED.
4. "Sorted" is a common English dictionary word — IP Australia distinctiveness threshold is hard to clear; examiners reject single-word common adjectives without proven secondary meaning.

**Domain reality:**
- sorted.com: **TAKEN** (since 1995, premium)
- sorted.com.au: **TAKEN** — Airtasker Limited (defensive)
- sortedapp.com: **TAKEN** — Namecheap, parked since 2013
- getsorted.com: **TAKEN** — since 1999, active EMEA hosting
- sorted.app: **TAKEN**

**Recommended alternative spellings if 🟡/🔴:**
- "GetSorted" — collides with existing 1999 domain.
- "SortedBroker" / "SortedHQ" — marginally cleaner but doesn't escape the Sorted.org.nz brand confusion in the ANZ finance category.
- Recommend dropping entirely.

**Brand-fit for Duncan's product:**
The word "Sorted" carries great mortgage-customer emotional payoff ("got it sorted") — but the NZ government already owns that exact emotional positioning in the ANZ finance education space. Launching against a 25-year-old beloved govt brand in the adjacent market is brand suicide.

1. **T.D. Noone Clothing Pty Ltd** owns **beau.com.au** — Class 25 (apparel) — relevance: **LOW** (different Nice class, no service-mark overlap with Class 36/42).
2. **Beau** is a common French loanword meaning "handsome/beautiful" — used as a name, an adjective, and across multiple boutique brands (cosmetics, hospitality, weddings). ATMOSS likely shows 15-30 hits across classes; need to verify class 9/36/42 specifically with a paid TM agent search.
3. No mortgage/fintech direct collision surfaced.
4. **beau.com** is held by Gandi — registered 1995, premium tier — likely 6-figure acquisition cost.

**Domain reality:**
- beau.com: **TAKEN** (premium, 1995)
- beau.com.au: **TAKEN** — T.D. Noone Clothing (apparel, different industry)
- beauapp.com: **TAKEN** — MarkMonitor (corporate brand-protection registrar; suggests a Fortune 500 holds it defensively, possibly L'Oréal or LVMH)
- getbeau.com: **TAKEN** — NameBright since 2018, likely parked investor
- beau.app: **TAKEN**

**Recommended alternative spellings if 🟡/🔴:**
- **"BeauHQ"**, **"BeauBroker"**, **"BeauFi"** — all cleaner; coined compound clears distinctiveness bar.
- **"MeetBeau.com"** or **"hello-beau.com.au"** are likely available and would let Duncan keep the name.

**Brand-fit for Duncan's product:**

**Brand-fit for Duncan's product:**

## 5. Lodgey

**Verdict: 🟢 Clear — Strong Candidate**

**Top conflicts found:**
1. **Lodgify** (vacation-rental software, Spain-HQ) — Class 9/42 — relevance: **LOW-MED**. Phonetically adjacent ("Lodge-ee-fy" vs "Lodge-ee"), but Lodgify is firmly in short-term-rental property mgmt — zero mortgage/finance overlap. Examiner would likely allow co-existence; risk is consumer confusion at scale.
2. **lodgey.com.au** is held by a private individual (Dylan John Reeves) on serverRenewProhibited — defensive hold, no active product. Likely acquirable for low 4-figures.
3. No ASIC company-name collision surfaced. "Lodgey" is a coined word — easy to clear the distinctiveness bar at IP Australia.
4. "Lodge" is the exact verb used in the AU mortgage industry ("lodge the application", "lodgement to lender") — strong descriptive linkage but with the "-ey" suffix, it transitions to a fanciful mark, which is the strongest TM category.

**Domain reality:**
- lodgey.com: **PARKED** (registered, no active site)
- lodgey.com.au: **TAKEN** — individual holder, defensive
- lodgeyapp.com: **AVAILABLE** (no registration record found)
- getlodgey.com: **AVAILABLE** (no registration record found)
- lodgey.app: **TAKEN** but no active site

**Recommended alternative spellings if 🟡/🔴:**
- Not needed. "Lodgey" itself is the cleanest of all 5 candidates.
- Fallback: **"Lodgr"** or **"LodgeIQ"** if .com.au acquisition fails.

**Brand-fit for Duncan's product:**
"Lodgey" is the only candidate where the *name does work for the product*. Mortgage brokers say "lodge the file" 30 times a day; "Lodgey" personifies the lodgement action as a helpful sidekick. It's coined, ownable, distinctive, descriptive-but-not-too-descriptive, and the entire AU broker industry will get the joke on first hearing.
