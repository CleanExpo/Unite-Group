Duncan contrasted it sharply with traditional home loans, particularly those with offset accounts, which he criticized as a \"clever marketing trick\" by banks to extend loan terms. The DIY Home Loan forces disciplined wealth creation through a set of 20 rules. It splits a client's mortgage into two facilities: a non-tax-deductible principal loan called a \"GISI\" (Goes In, Stays In) account with no redraw or offset features, and a separate, tax-deductible line of credit for investment.\n\nThe product's rules mandate that clients create and adhere to a quarterly budget. Any savings achieved (e.g., $1,000 per month) are directed into the GISI loan. The resulting available redraw is then mandatorily transferred into superannuation as a concessional contribution, generating a tax deduction. Simultaneously, an equivalent amount is drawn from the investment facility and invested into diversified, high-growth assets like ETFs. He predicts this will push investors to sell existing properties and seek more sophisticated strategies.\n\nThese strategies may include complex inter-family wealth transfers\u2014such as parents helping children purchase homes to access tax-free capital gains\u2014or developing new properties to benefit from new-build incentives.
