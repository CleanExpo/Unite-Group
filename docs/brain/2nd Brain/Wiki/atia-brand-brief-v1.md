## 1. Brand mission

The **Australian Trade Industry Association (ATIA)** is the meta-body for Australian trade services. It exists to set and enforce standards across six trade verticals — Restoration, Carpet Cleaning, Indoor Environmental Professionals (IEP), Plumbing, HVAC, and Pressure Washing — covering both Home and Business work.

ATIA's four functions:

1. **Standards** — codify what "competent" looks like in each vertical.
2. **Certification** — accredit individual practitioners against those standards.
3. **Practitioner accreditation** — maintain the public register of certified tradespeople.
4. **Supplier validation** — vet and badge the equipment, chemistry and software suppliers the industry uses.

The target is recognition as Australia's authoritative standards body for trade services by **Q3 2026**.

## 2. Naming risk — read this first

The IP Australia trade-mark register (searched 2026-05-14) shows a live, registered **"ATIA — Australian Travel Industry Association"** mark held by the Australian Federation of Travel Agents (TM 2382092 + 2515428, classes 9, 39, 41, 42). Travel and trade-services are different industries — there is a defensible argument that the marks are non-confusing — but the acronym overlap is real and a v1 brand identity needs to lean on the **full wordmark** rather than the acronym alone for at least the first 12 months.

**Design implication:** the wordmark "AUSTRALIAN TRADE INDUSTRY ASSOCIATION" is the primary lockup. "ATIA" only appears as a secondary mark.

## 3. Brand personality

| Attribute | Yes | No |
|---|---|---|
| Tone | Authoritative, practical, plain-spoken | Stuffy, academic, ceremonial |
| Posture | Standards-setter, peer-elected | Regulator, bureaucracy |
| Aesthetic | Distinctly Australian, working-class respect | UK Royal Society, US Ivy League |
| Audience read | A 50-something tradie sees the mark and trusts it | A graphic designer thinks it looks "clever" |

ATIA is a trade body run **for** tradies. The visual identity must read instantly as Australian, professional, and earned. Not corporate, not government, not tech.

## 5. Colour palette — three directions to choose from

### Direction A — Traditional trade
- Deep workshop blue (e.g. `#0F2A44`)
- Rust orange accent (e.g. `#C2410C`)
- Reads as: hardware-store, reliable, established
- Risk: too close to "industrial supply chain" brands

### Direction B — Modern professional
- Navy (e.g. `#0B1F3A`)
- Warm grey (e.g. `#A89B8C`)
- Single warm accent (e.g. burnt amber `#B45309`)
- Reads as: consulting body, contemporary, considered
- Risk: too close to corporate / financial-services aesthetic

### Direction C — Australian native
- Eucalypt green (e.g. `#3E5641`)
- Ochre (e.g. `#B7791F`)
- Warm sand neutral (e.g. `#E7D9C2`)
- Reads as: distinctly Australian, earthy, place-rooted
- Risk: must avoid leaning into First-Nations-coded design — this is a trade body, not a cultural body

**Recommendation:** Direction B for the formal seal + cert work. Direction C for marketing and conference identity. Avoid Direction A unless paired with a strong modern type system.

---

## 6. Typography — two directions

### Direction 1 — Neutral, contemporary
- Body: **Inter** or **Söhne**
- Display: same family, heavy weight, tight tracking on the wordmark
- Reads as: clean, modern, professional

### Direction 2 — Authority serif
- Display / wordmark: a **transitional serif** (e.g. Tiempos Headline, Source Serif, or a slab like Roboto Slab if budget is tight)
- Body: a quiet sans (Inter / Söhne)
- Accent: **Söhne Mono** for cert numbers, member IDs, technical callouts
- Reads as: institutional, earned, considered

**Recommendation:** Direction 2. A serif wordmark signals authority and longevity in a way a sans cannot, and differentiates ATIA from every tech-startup trade-services product on the market.

## 7. Inspiration references (study these)

1. **Master Builders Australia** — masterbuilders.com.au — wordmark + state-chapter lockup pattern is exactly the structure ATIA needs.
2. **Housing Industry Association (HIA)** — hia.com.au — strong red, decades-old equity; study how the acronym carries.
3. **Restoration Industry Association (US)** — restorationindustry.org — the US sister body; useful for cert-seal language.
4. **CORE Restoration (US)** — gowithcore.com — the covert template Phill is modelling DR on; study tone, photography, member-first narrative.
5. **Carpet Cleaners Warehouse (CCW)** — existing logo — first paying client; ATIA must feel like it could endorse CCW without either brand swallowing the other.

## 8. Anti-references — do NOT look like

- Tech startup (no rounded-corner gradient logos)
- Government department (no Commonwealth Coat-of-Arms-adjacent crests)
- Real-estate franchise (no rectangular badge with surname inside)
- MLM (no swooshy aspirational marks)
- Trade union (no clenched fists, no banner motifs)

---

## 9. Constraints (hard requirements)

- **Single-colour print** — the cert seal must work in 100% black, 100% white-on-dark, and one-colour foil
- **Favicon legibility** — secondary mark must be recognisable at 32×32 px
- **Scale ceiling** — primary wordmark must work at 3m banner height without losing form
- **Photocopier-safe** — A4 member certificate must survive a 200-DPI black-and-white photocopy and still be identifiable
- **Australian content** — no UK royal imagery, no US-style eagle / shield, no European heraldry
