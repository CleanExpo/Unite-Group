## Industry Landscape

The building science sector requires deep technical knowledge, bridging construction practices with environmental health. Key players include:
- **Consultants:** Providing specialized advice on air leakage, moisture, and material science.
- **Builders:** Implementing best practices in construction and retrofitting.
- **Regulators:** Establishing minimum standards for building performance.

## Content Pillars

Our content strategy focuses on three core pillars:
1. **Prevention:** Best practices for new construction and retrofitting to minimize moisture and air leakage.
2. **Mitigation:** Scientific approaches to managing mold, VOCs, and other indoor air pollutants.
3. **Resilience:** Designing buildings to withstand extreme weather and environmental changes.

## Appendix: Key Concepts

**Air Tightness:** The measure of how effectively a building envelope prevents uncontrolled air leakage.
**Moisture Management:** Controlling the movement and accumulation of water within a structure to prevent mold and structural damage.
**VOCs (Volatile Organic Compounds):** Chemicals emitted from building materials that can negatively impact indoor air quality.
**Building Envelope:** The physical barrier that separates the conditioned interior from the unconditioned exterior.
