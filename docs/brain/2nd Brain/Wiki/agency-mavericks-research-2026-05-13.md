# Agency Mavericks — Research Brief (2026-05-13)

## TL;DR

Troy Dean (Agency Mavericks, 15 years coaching digital agencies) is publicly proving a **"headless agency"** thesis: **28 clients × $3,000/mo = $1M ARR at ~10 hours/week, solo + AI**, with a 4-week run to 10 clients already banked. The mechanic is a **library of AI "skills"** (brand-voice extraction, ICP/strategy, 1.5–2k-word weekly blog, 40 social posts/mo, weekly newsletter, image gen, **AI-run Asana + Slack**) glued together by MCP-era platform APIs (Meta, Salesforce, etc.), with GoHighLevel as CRM. This is **the working proof point for [[pathway-to-2b-2026-2028]] Pillar 2** — the in-house Synthex marketing engine — but Troy's economics (28 high-volume B2B agency clients, no ad spend stated, productised commodity content) only **partially** map onto Unite-Group's vetted-clients-only, $2B-exit, $200M-ARR thesis.

## The Agency Mavericks model — extracted verbatim

### Client count / pricing / packaging

> "28 clients at $3,000 a month each. That's $84,000 a month in recurring revenue, $1,8,000 a year [sic — $1.008M]."
> — Part 1, line 81

- Target: **28 clients × $3K/mo = $1M ARR** (Part 1, line 75; Part 2, line 50)
- Current state at recording (Part 2, ~2026-05-11): **10 paying clients, from zero 4 weeks prior** (Part 1, line 73; Part 2, line 50)
- Onboarding cadence: **1 new client per week, by choice** (Part 1, line 73; Part 2, line 50)
- Setup labor: **90 days upfront** (Part 1, line 84; Part 2, line 142)
- Steady-state: **~90 min/client/month × 28 = 42 hr/mo ÷ 4.3 wks = 9.7 hr/wk** (Part 1, line 83)
- ICP: **Troy's own clients are digital agency owners** ("my clients are digital agency owners. They make content for a living") — Part 1, line 137; Part 2, line 111
- Capped at 28 by lifestyle choice; will not scale to 200+ (Part 3, lines 67, 125)

### Delivery workflow (the 10-hour-a-week claim)

Per-client monthly output (Part 1, lines 86–94):

| Deliverable | Cadence | Spec |
|---|---|---|
| Long-form thought-leadership blog | Weekly | 1,500–2,000 words, keyword-optimised for Google + LLM search, branded featured image |
| Email newsletter | Weekly | Sent to client's list, tied to content strategy |
| Social media posts | ~40/month | Each with unique generated image bound to client style guide |
| Brand voice guide | Once at onboarding | From discovery call transcript |
| ICP + 3-month content strategy | Once at onboarding | Pillars, post titles, social themes, keyword research |

Onboarding cycle (Part 2, lines 90–98):

- **Week 1 — Discovery:** 60-min kickoff call. AI listens, researches website + socials, produces brand voice + ICP + content strategy docs.
- **Week 2 — Strategy build:** 3-month content map, image guide, email sequences. 30-min approval call.
- **Week 3 — Production starts:** Troy heavy-QCs first batch, feeds corrections back into the system.
- **Weeks 4–8 — Calibration:** QC pass rate climbs; revisions and errors decline.
- **Weeks 9–12 — Autonomous operation:** "The AI is handling everything else. Production, project management, client communications" (Part 2, line 98).

What stays human (Part 2, line 120):
1. **Sales / networking / relationships** — "Humans buy from humans."
2. **Strategic 20–30 min calls × 2/month per client.**
3. **System supervision** — QC pass rate, training new skills.

> "The AI is in Asana. You're not." — Part 2, line 126

### Tooling stack

Troy's stated stack (Part 2, line 131):

| Function | Tool |
|---|---|
| Project management | **Asana** (or ClickUp — "fine") |
| CRM | **GoHighLevel** ("clunky as f*** but it does the job") |
| Comms (client + AI) | **Slack** — dedicated channel per client; "our AI is built for Slack" |
| Publishing | WordPress |
| Productivity | Google Workspace |
| Ads | Meta (post-MCP release) |

> "Total monthly cost is in the hundreds, not the thousands. It's way less than one employee." — Part 2, line 132

The "AI" is **NOT ChatGPT / Claude / Gemini as a chatbot.** It is a **system of specialised agents** ("skills"), each documented and tested, plugged together. (Part 2, lines 54–66.)

> "Skill files are the infrastructure. Think of a skill file as a modern version of a standard operating procedure. Instead of writing an SOP and delegating that to a human being to follow, you're writing a skills file and giving that to the AI." — Part 2, line 135

The seven named skills (Part 2, lines 71–80):
1. Brand voice extraction
2. ICP + content strategy
3. Long-form blog writing
4. Social media production
5. Email newsletter
6. **Project management (Asana auto-updated)**
7. **Client communication (Slack auto-updated)**

Underlying thesis: **MCP (Model Context Protocol)** turns every major platform (Salesforce, Meta, Anthropic, OpenAI, Stripe, Notion) headless. Per-seat SaaS pricing dies; per-action pricing replaces it (Part 1, lines 111–127).

### Client acquisition motion

**Inbound only — and Troy is explicit it is NOT part of what he sells.**

- "We're not promising leads here and we're not closing deals for you." (Part 2, line 140)
- "Our system makes your delivery fast. It doesn't make your phone ring." (Part 3, line 67)
- His own 10 clients came from **his existing coaching audience** ("my own paying customers asked me to package it" — Part 1, line 70).
- Single funnel asset: **Facebook post → waitlist (150+ opt-ins) → 3-video series → application call** (Part 1, lines 63, 161; Part 3, line 47).
- Required of buyers: **existing network, sales chops, ability to have a strategic conversation with a CEO** (Part 3, line 64).

### Productized service catalogue

Troy delivers **content + comms only.** Out of scope (stated or implied):
- No ad campaign management (mentioned as future skill via Meta MCP — Part 2, line 80)
- No web design / dev
- No e-commerce / Shopify
- No "big API integration company" custom work (Part 1, line 95)

> "I'm not running ads for e-commerce... I'm not building custom hand-crafted websites for big API integration companies. We're doing productized service delivery here." — Part 1, line 95

The two productised offers Troy sells **about** the model (Part 3, lines 78–90):
1. **Headless Lab** — 90-day group cohort, weekly call, private Slack, full skill library. Founding cohort pricing (specific price not disclosed in videos).
2. **Headless Install** — 90-day 1:1 done-for-you. Two spots left at recording. Price rises when full.

Guarantee: **"value-back" not money-back.** "I'll just keep working until you've got it." (Part 3, line 100)

## Risks + traps

1. **Volume conflict with vetted-clients rule.** Troy's math requires 28 paying clients. [[pathway-to-2b-2026-2028]] says quality > quantity. The model only fits Unite-Group if **per-client ARR is materially higher than $3K/mo** so 5–10 vetted clients hit the same revenue line. Direct copy = breaks the vet gate.
2. **Lifestyle cap vs $2B exit.** Troy is 52 and optimising for school pickups and Tuesday guitar. Phill is targeting a $200M ARR strategic acquisition. Same operating model; **opposite end states.** Adopting Troy's pacing (1 new client/week) does not get to $200M ARR by June 2028.
3. **Slack-shaped infrastructure.** Troy's "AI is built for Slack" claim is a hard architectural dependency. We are Telegram-only by [[feedback_no_slack]]. Replicating his client-comms skill on Telegram needs deliberate engineering, not just a tool swap.
4. **GoHighLevel competitive trap.** GHL is a CRM-platform-marketed-to-agencies. We sell [[unite-crm]] to the same market. Recommending GHL would be marketing our own competitor. Hard no.
5. **Niche assumption.** Troy's 10 clients are *all digital agency owners* — he sells to the audience that already trusts him. Unite-Group's ICP is vetted property-services / restoration / cleaning firms via [[industry-association-vision-2026]]. The acquisition motion does not transfer.
6. **No ads inside the model is unverified.** Troy says he runs no ads for his agency clients but does mention "Meta MCP" as a future skill, and his own funnel is a Facebook post — he likely advertises that post. Need to verify before claiming "Troy proves no-ad-spend works at $1M ARR."
7. **Output is content-only.** Troy delivers no product, no SaaS, no association, no IICRC compliance, no IAQ E.E.A.T. — his $1M is **commodity content fulfilment.** Our $200M ARR thesis includes 7 product surfaces. The model is a fulfilment-layer proof, not a strategy proof.
8. **90-day setup window is real and gates ROI.** Troy is honest about this. For each vetted Unite-Group client, expect 90 days before autonomous operation. Plan for that in customer-success commitments.

## Open questions for the Senior PM

1. **Pricing translation:** At what ARR per vetted client does Troy's model scale to $200M ARR by June 2028 with ≤50 clients total across the portfolio? (Working math: $200M / 50 = **$4M ARR per vetted client**, or $333K/mo — 100× Troy's price point. Is that achievable via Member-Services Stack + SaaS bundle + association partnership, or do we need a different denominator?)
2. **Skill-library gap audit:** Which of Troy's seven named skills (brand voice / ICP / blog / social / newsletter / PM / client comms) do we already have at production quality vs. need to build? Specifically — is there an equivalent of skill #7 (client-comms autopilot on Telegram) shipped, or is that the open gap to close?
3. **Slack → Telegram architectural translation:** Troy's "per-client dedicated Slack channel where AI posts updates and handles revision requests" — what is the minimum-viable Telegram-bot equivalent, and does it need its own per-client Telegram group, or can we multiplex through the existing channel + chat_id partitioning?
4. **Should we publish the "headless agency" thesis under [[synthex]]?** Troy is building a public-facing audience around this playbook. We have a deeper version. Is there a Pillar 5 (Visibility) opportunity to position Synthex publicly as **the** $2B-grade headless agency — and use that as part of the strategic-acquirer narrative for the [[exit-thesis]] pipeline? (See [[seo-linkable-assets]] for related linkable-asset pattern.)
5. **Vetted-client onboarding template:** Troy's 4-stage onboarding (discovery → strategy → production → calibration over 12 weeks) is cleaner than what we have documented in [[marketing-agency-blueprint-2026]]. Do we port his 4-week phase model verbatim into our vetted-client intake SOP, with [[brand-guardian]] + [[qa-lead]] gates inserted at each phase boundary?
