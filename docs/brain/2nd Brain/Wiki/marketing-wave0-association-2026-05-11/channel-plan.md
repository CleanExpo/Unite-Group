# Channel Plan — Expanded NRPG Industry Association (Wave 0 Launch)

> **INTERNAL — Unite-Group portfolio only — do not distribute publicly 2026-05-11**

Skill: `marketing-channel-strategist` · Wave 2 · job `nrpg-association-wave0-2026-05-11`.
Reads: [[positioning-doc]] · [[icp-research]] · `Synthex/packages/brand-config/src/brands/nrpg.ts` · `Synthex/packages/brand-config/src/brands/john-coutis.ts` · wave-plan JSON.
Wave-0 channels (locked by brief): `linkedin`, `youtube`, `web`.
Wave-0 media posture (locked by brief): organic-only, no paid spend until Wave 1.

The channel mix is **ICP-driven, not Coutis-driven**. The ICP research is clear: 10-25% of the trade ICP recognises Coutis by name, 3-5% are genuine fans. That makes Coutis a halo signal, not the trust hook. The weekly trust-building work runs on LinkedIn through Phill, Toby, and the founding-partner cadence. Coutis carries the YouTube anchor asset and shows up in cross-cut LinkedIn video — but the channel plan refuses to over-rotate on him.

## Channel Ranking (by ICP-fit, not by Coutis-fit)

Ranked by realistic Wave-0 reach × conversion path × cost-to-execute. Coutis-led reach is treated as a halo bonus only.

### 1. LinkedIn — PRIMARY trust channel

- **ICP-reach estimate:** ~70-80% of the primary ICP. Owner-operators of 5-50-employee firms have LinkedIn accounts even if dormant; the ICP research names two LinkedIn groups explicitly ("RIA APAC Chapter", "IICRC Australia Network") plus the broader "Australasian Carpet Cleaners Network".
- **Conversion path:** post → profile click → founding-partners landing page → interest signup. Founder-level posts from Phill (and Toby once available) outperform brand-page posts on LinkedIn 8-12×; the algorithm rewards personal accounts.
- **Why primary:** highest-density platform for B2B trade owners in ANZ. Phill is already posting infrequently on LinkedIn — Wave 0 is a cadence step-up, not a cold start. Aligns with NRPG voice (`authoritative + expert`).
- **Realistic Wave-0 conversion ceiling:** 30-50 signups from LinkedIn alone, assuming 5 launch-week posts averaging 2-4k impressions each + founding-partner amplification.

### 2. Web (unitegroup.in/association landing page) — CONVERSION CHANNEL

- **ICP-reach estimate:** ~100% of qualified traffic (because traffic only arrives via the other two channels). Not a discovery channel — it's the conversion floor.
- **Conversion path:** any-channel CTA → landing page → email capture / interest list / founder-tier waitlist. This is the single source of truth for the "≥50 interest signups by 2026-05-31" gate.
- **Why second:** every other channel funnels here. If the page is wrong, the campaign fails regardless of channel reach.
- **Wave-0 deliverable:** one landing page at `unitegroup.in/association` (or sub-path), one signup form, three CTAs matched to the four pricing tiers (Founder $249 cap 100, Firm $1,200, Enterprise $2,500+, Sole-trader $79).
- **SEO note:** organic search reach for "ANZ restoration association" / "Australian restoration peak body" is the late-Wave-0 sleeper. Page must be indexable on launch day so peer-search triggers (per ICP Decision Process step 2) land here, not on a competitor.

### 3. YouTube — HALO + ASSET HOSTING

- **ICP-reach estimate:** ~15-25% of the primary ICP organically (low). Most trade owners watch YouTube tutorials (psychrometry walkthroughs, drying log explainers) but do not subscribe to industry channels. YouTube's role in Wave-0 is **asset hosting + cross-cut distribution**, not standalone discovery.
- **Conversion path:** LinkedIn post embeds YouTube video → viewer clicks through to YouTube → description-CTA drives to landing page. YouTube is a *waypoint*, not the destination.
- **Why third:** the 1k-views-in-7d gate is achievable only because the video is *cross-posted to LinkedIn natively*. Native LinkedIn video reach + the embedded YouTube cross-post + Coutis's existing audience (corporate-keynote crossover) hits the threshold. Pure-organic-YouTube discovery does not.
- **Wave-0 deliverable:** one 60-75s Coutis intro video, optimised for both LinkedIn-native (1920×1080) and YouTube hosting.

## Watering-Hole Engagement Plan

Per ICP research, three watering holes matter most for Wave 0. Each requires a non-spammy presence play.

### 1. IAQ Magazine Australia — Phill's editorial-committee seat (UNFAIR ADVANTAGE)

- **Play:** Phill pitches a single editorial column to IAQ Magazine Australia titled "The peak body that runs jobs — why the ANZ restoration industry stopped waiting for an import" (or similar, owned by `marketing-copywriter`). Column references the expanded NRPG association in context, links to landing page in the author bio.
- **Why this works:** Phill already holds the editorial committee seat (per `industry-association-vision-2026`). Pitching is internal not cold. IAQ readership overlaps with the IAQ/restoration ICP at a higher density than any other AU trade publication.
- **Wave-0 action:** Pitch by Wed 13 May (D2). Editor's response window 3-7 days. Target publication: next monthly IAQ issue (mid-June, slips to Wave 1+ but the pitch and announcement are Wave-0 events).
- **Sub-skill owner:** `marketing-copywriter` drafts the editorial pitch + 600-800 word column.
- **Forbidden:** No abuse of the editorial seat for direct association advertising. The column must be a genuine industry-perspective piece; the association mention is contextual. Trust-chain integrity matters more than any single placement.

### 2. LinkedIn industry groups (RIA APAC Chapter, IICRC Australia Network, Australasian Carpet Cleaners Network)

- **Play:** Phill (personal account) joins each group if not already a member. Posts one *non-promotional* contribution per group during launch week — a question, a poll, or a short observation on the trade. The association is NOT mentioned in-group; the group serves as a discoverability surface for Phill's personal LinkedIn profile where the launch content lives.
- **Why this works:** Direct association promotion in trade groups reads as spam and trips group-admin removals. Personal-profile lift via in-group engagement drives organic discovery; the launch posts on Phill's personal feed are then found by group-members who clicked through.
- **Wave-0 action:** D-1 to D0 (Sunday-Monday) join + lurk. D1-D3 contribute non-promotional content. D5-D7 measure group-traffic-to-profile-views via LinkedIn analytics.
- **Sub-skill owner:** `marketing-social-content` drafts the in-group contributions. Phill posts personally — no brand-page activity in third-party groups.

### 3. Facebook Restoration Trade Talk Australia (closed group)

- **Play:** No direct posting by Phill (he is not an existing trusted node in the group). **Toby Bredhauer** posts. Toby is the CCW national equipment distribution founder — he already has a credible presence with the membership. Toby posts a peer-to-peer announcement: "Joined as a founding partner of an expanded NRPG industry association — here's why" → links to landing page.
- **Why this works:** Decision Process step 4 ("peer check") is the conversion pivot. Toby's voice in this group is the peer signal the rest of the ICP needs. Phill or a brand voice posting cold would read as marketing intrusion.
- **Wave-0 action:** D3 (Thursday 14 May) — synced with Toby's LinkedIn amplification post. Same message, two channels, two voices.
- **Sub-skill owner:** `marketing-copywriter` ghost-writes for Toby; Toby reviews and posts personally.
- **Risk:** group admin may remove. Mitigation: Toby asks the admin in advance (one DM); if blocked, Toby posts to "Aussie Carpet Cleaners" and "Water Damage Restoration Australia" instead.

## Coutis Channel Role — Halo, Not Trust Hook

The ICP research is unambiguous: 10-25% name-recognition in the trade ICP, 3-5% genuine fans. The channel plan reflects this by:

1. **Coutis carries ONE asset in Wave 0** — the 75s intro video. He is not the voice of every post, the host of every channel, or the named "founder" of the association on the landing page. He is the **spokesman**, on-camera and on the YouTube channel.
2. **Trust-building weekly cadence runs on LinkedIn through Phill and Toby**, not Coutis. Phill is the founder voice; Toby is the peer voice; Coutis is the public-recognition voice.
3. **Coutis-led channels (his YouTube subscribers, his keynote-circuit audience) are halo distribution** — useful for the "this is bigger than a trade side-project" credibility marker, NOT for ICP penetration. The 1,000-views-in-7d gate is hit by LinkedIn-cross-post traffic, not by his organic YouTube subscriber base.
4. **Wave 1+ relaxes this constraint as Coutis content compounds.** The Rolling Success podcast relaunch (per Wave 1+ deliverables) lets Coutis carry weekly content rhythm; until then, he is a halo asset.
5. **Downstream copywriter instruction:** do NOT lead LinkedIn launch-thread copy with a Coutis quote. Lead with Pain #1 or Pain #3 + the founding-partner reveal. Coutis appears in the *proof-point bank* (positioning-doc), not the *hook*.

This downgrade is the load-bearing decision of the channel plan. If a downstream skill (copywriter, social-content) drifts back to Coutis-as-hook framing, this section overrides.

## Wave-0 Measurement Plan

Three success gates per brief. Each maps to specific channels:

| Gate | Target | Deadline | Channel(s) responsible | Tracking mechanism |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Coutis engagement contract signed | 1 | 2026-05-25 (D14) | Off-channel (founder direct) — but visibility via YouTube + LinkedIn video drop legitimises the offer | Linear ticket / signed contract scan |
| First Coutis video views | ≥1,000 in 7d | D1+7d = 2026-05-19 (D8) | LinkedIn native cross-post (primary driver) + YouTube hosting (secondary) | LinkedIn analytics + YouTube analytics, summed |
| Interest-list signups | ≥50 | 2026-05-31 (D20) | LinkedIn (primary discovery) → Web (conversion) | Supabase `interest_signups` row count + UTM attribution by source |

**Attribution model** (handed to `marketing-analytics-attribution` Wave 4):
- UTM scheme: `?utm_source={channel}&utm_medium={asset-type}&utm_campaign=nrpg-wave0-2026-05&utm_content={post-id}`
- Per-channel UTM examples: `utm_source=linkedin&utm_medium=post-phill-launch`, `utm_source=youtube&utm_medium=video-description`, `utm_source=facebook&utm_medium=group-toby-trade-talk`
- Last-non-direct-click attribution as default. Founder-call signups (Toby's WhatsApp peer outreach) attributed via `utm_source=peer-referral` on landing-page form referrer field.
- Reporting cadence: daily dashboard from D1; founder-review checkpoint D7 + D14 + D20.

**Failure modes + escalation:**
- If D8 video views < 500 → escalate to `marketing-orchestrator`; consider paid boost authorisation (would break Wave-0 organic-only constraint — founder override required).
- If D14 signups < 20 → trigger Wave-1 channel expansion early (email outreach to known peers, podcast pitch acceleration).
- If Coutis contract slips past D14 → re-frame all subsequent LinkedIn copy to remove "Hosted by John Coutis OAM" language; substitute "Backed by founding partners across the industry" until resolved.
