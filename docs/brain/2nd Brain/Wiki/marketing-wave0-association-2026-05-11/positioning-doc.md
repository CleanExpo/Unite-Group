## Value Proposition (one sentence)

For an ANZ restoration or cleaning firm owner running a 5-to-50-person crew, the expanded NRPG association is a Member-as-a-Service body that bundles cert, leads, marketing, software, insurance, and equipment into one monthly fee — turning a peak-body badge into the operating system of the business.

> April Dunford long-form: *For the ANZ restoration/cleaning firm owner who is paying for cert, software, marketing, leads, and equipment from six different suppliers, the expanded NRPG association is a Member-as-a-Service body that bundles every supplier into one membership. Unlike IICRC (cert only), RIA (US-led advocacy only), or Prime Creative (publishing only), NRPG routes paid jobs to certified firms and runs the marketing for them.*

## JTBD — Jobs-to-be-Done

Each job is phrased in Christensen "When / I want to / so I can" form. Five jobs in priority order.

1. **Get more profitable jobs in the diary.**
   When a quiet week shows up on the calendar, the firm owner wants to know paid work will arrive without buying Google Ads at AUD $80 a click, so the business stops living job-to-job.

2. **Prove the firm is the safe choice without becoming a sales operation.**
   When an insurer, broker, or builder is choosing between three firms, the owner wants a live trust mark (cert current today, last-5-jobs scored, insurance current) instead of a static yearly badge, so the firm wins on credibility, not the lowest quote.

3. **Stop running marketing, IT, and admin out of the foreman's ute.**
   When the foreman is the website manager, the bookkeeper, and the social-media intern at 9pm Tuesday, the owner wants the back office handled by people who do it for a living, so the trades crew can stay on the tools.

4. **Keep techs certified without losing them to a week in a Las Vegas hotel.**
   When a tech needs WRT, AMRT, or fire-and-smoke, the owner wants AU-delivered, IICRC-recognised training that does not cost a flight, hotel, and a week of lost site time, so cert renewals are not a budget event.

5. **Have a peak body that actually argues with the insurer.**
   When an insurer kicks back a legitimate scope or pays at 2018 rates, the owner wants a national body that turns up at the table on the firm's behalf, so a single 22-person crew is not negotiating with a 4,000-person claims operation alone.

## Competitive positioning

The expanded NRPG association is positioned **against six archetypes** sourced from [[competitor-service-stack-2026-05-11]]. The competitor scan is comprehensive; this section reads from it rather than re-researching.

### Direct competitor — IICRC

| Dimension | IICRC | Expanded NRPG association |
|---|---|---|
| Origin | Las Vegas; global standards body | ANZ-native; member-business operator |
| Product | Technician cert + Certified Firm + ANSI standards | All of the above, **plus** the operating system around the cert |
| Standards velocity | ~5 years to ANSI publish | 60-day Best Practice Briefs (intentionally not ANSI — designed for speed) |
| Insurer recognition | Universal | Inherited via partnership, not replaced |
| Strategic posture | Partner, not displace | IICRC certs continue inside the NRPG stack; CARSI becomes IICRC Approved School |

The contest is not "NRPG vs IICRC." The contest is "NRPG with IICRC inside it" vs. "IICRC alone, in a US-time-zone, that the ANZ firm has to wrap their own operations around."

### Indirect competitor — RIA US (and its ANZ chapter)

RIA's mode is membership + AGA advocacy + Convention. The ANZ chapter is small (count not publicly disclosed; per [[competitor-service-stack-2026-05-11]]). Position: RIA tries to *advocate* on insurer equity; the expanded NRPG association *routes the jobs* through the DR platform. Advocacy without job-routing is a 20-year project that produces position papers. Job-routing produces invoices.

### Indirect competitor — Prime Creative Media (and Intermedia, BNP, Endeavor)

These are publishers. Their product is the reader's attention, sold to advertisers. The expanded NRPG association is **not in the publishing business** — Prime Creative has 30 years of trade-press infrastructure that is uneconomic to replicate. Position: NRPG partners with IAQ Magazine Australia for editorial credibility (Phill's existing seat is the wedge), uses Coutis for owned-video reach, and skips print-magazine building entirely.

### Indirect competitor — ISSA

ISSA's hub model (10 industry-specific community hubs) is the right structural reference for the year-3 federation play. ISSA does **not** offer marketing-as-a-service, lead routing, or AI tooling for member firms. Position: NRPG copies the hub structure post-launch, never matches ISSA's mega-expo, beats ISSA on operating-system depth from day one.

### Indirect competitor — AIRAH, HIA, FMA, MBA (other AU peak bodies)

These bodies are trade-adjacent (HVAC, residential construction, facility management, commercial construction). None is restoration-shaped. Position: NRPG is the missing peak body for the **damage-response trades** — water, fire, mould, storm, hard-floor, IEP. The COSBOA affiliation in Wave 2 brings federal-policy adjacency without competing.

### "Doing nothing" alternative

The dominant competitor is **inertia** — firms paying $4–8k a year across six suppliers for what NRPG bundles for less. The job is to make the switching cost lower than the status quo carrying cost. The Member-as-a-Service price-point makes that a one-conversation decision.

## Category creation — is "Member-as-a-Service" a new category?

**Honest answer: it is a new entry in the "industry association" frame customers arrive with, NOT a Day-1 category creation.**

The reasoning:

- **For:** No incumbent in ANZ or globally bundles cert + leads + marketing + software + insurance + equipment into a single monthly fee. The competitor scan shows zero ticks in that row of the matrix.
- **Against:** Tradespeople do not arrive at the conversation saying "find me Member-as-a-Service." They arrive saying "find me a peak body that actually does something." That is the existing category — industry association. Trying to invent a new label on day one adds friction; the customer has to learn the category before evaluating the product.

**Strategic call:** lead with the familiar frame ("industry association"), and let the product behaviour ("everything you need to run a firm, in one membership") create the category over 18–24 months. Year-3, once the operating-system bundle is the dominant retention driver, formally name the category in editorial — "Member-as-a-Service" or a better-tested label. Premature category-creation is a Dunford anti-pattern; product-led category-creation works.

For internal documents (founding-partners memo, investor decks), "Member-as-a-Service" is the *moat language*. For external launch copy, the frame is "the industry association that runs your business with you."

The strategy doc calls this the **$12M-ARR moat** (1,000 members × $1k/month bundle). The tradie translation:

> Most peak bodies charge a few hundred bucks a year for a directory listing and an email newsletter. The expanded NRPG association replaces the six suppliers a restoration firm already pays — the marketing agency, the IT contractor, the lead-gen company, the cert renewal, the insurance broker, the equipment account — with one monthly fee, paid once. The price-point is set so the membership pays for itself in the first job per quarter, every quarter.

## Tagline candidates (3)

Constraints: ≤7 words, Grade 5 readability, no forbidden pronouns (We/Our/I/Us/My), NRPG voice (authoritative + expert), do not present NRPG as regulatory.

### A. **"The peak body that runs jobs."**
- Words: 6. Reading level: Grade 4.
- Pronouns: clean.
- Why it works: collides "peak body" (institutional) with "runs jobs" (operational) in one breath. Reads to a tradie as "this one actually does something." Carries the Member-as-a-Service moat without using the phrase.

### B. **"Standards. Leads. Tools. One membership."**
- Words: 5. Reading level: Grade 4.
- Pronouns: clean.
- Why it works: literal bundle made readable. "Standards" preserves the existing `BrandConfig.tagline` lineage ("Standards for the response network."); "leads" + "tools" are the new pillars; "one membership" is the moat in two words. Strongest as a sub-tagline or chyron.

### C. **"Built for firms doing the work."**
- Words: 6. Reading level: Grade 3.
- Pronouns: clean.
- Why it works: positions against insurer-speak, against US imports, against publisher fluff. Earns trust by being the most boring sentence in the room. Risk: too generic — does not carry the bundle moat on its own.

### Lead with: **A — "The peak body that runs jobs."**

Reasons:
1. Names the category the audience arrives with ("peak body") so no learning curve is needed.
2. The verb "runs" does the differentiation work — every other peak body *advocates*, *certifies*, or *publishes*. None *runs jobs*.
3. Plain English. A tradie reads it once and understands it. A loss adjuster reads it once and understands it.
4. Tagline B becomes the workhorse sub-line on landing-page hero + founding-partners deck cover; tagline C is reserved for member-spotlight chyrons and Coutis-hosted CTAs.

## Anti-positioning — what NRPG is NOT

(Per skill spec — prevents copywriter drift.)

- **NOT a regulatory body.** NRPG sets industry standards. Government licenses trades. The association does not pretend to be the government. (Hard rule from `BrandConfig.doNot`.)
- **NOT a publisher.** Prime Creative, Intermedia, BNP, Endeavor own the publishing seat. NRPG partners with IAQ Magazine Australia for print and runs owned video — it does not chase a masthead empire.
- **NOT a Las-Vegas-style mega-expo. Not yet.** The conference is the flagship event. ISSA Show is a year-3+ aspiration; year-1 is one conference + state nights.
- **NOT a consumer-facing body.** The audience is firm owners and operators, not homeowners. Coutis is the spokesman, not a homeowner-channel personality.
- **NOT motivational-speaker programming.** Coutis hosts the channel because of national recognition and on-camera competence. The content is the industry conversation; Coutis is the chair, not the inspiration.
- **NOT a 1990s membership model.** Annual dues + directory listing is the category being disrupted, not the product being shipped.
