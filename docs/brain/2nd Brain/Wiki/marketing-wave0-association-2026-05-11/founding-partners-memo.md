# Founding Partners Memo — Expanded NRPG Industry Association

> **INTERNAL — Unite-Group portfolio only — do not distribute publicly 2026-05-11**

> Read this before saying yes to founding-member status. Built for industry-savvy operators. No marketing veneer.

---

## 1. Manifesto

The ANZ property-services industry has been paying six suppliers to do the work of one peak body. Cert from the US. Software from Sydney. Marketing from a freelancer in Bali. Leads from a directory site charging by the lock. Insurance from a broker who has never set foot on a fire job. Equipment from whoever picks up the phone. The expanded NRPG association ends that arrangement. One body. One membership. Standards held to the highest international benchmark, jobs routed to certified firms, marketing run by the people who built the platform, training delivered in Australian time zones, equipment supplied through national distribution, advocacy seated at the insurer table. Built for firms doing the work. Hosted by John Coutis OAM.

---

## 2. The problem (60 seconds for a busy operator)

A 5-50 FTE ANZ restoration or specialty-cleaning firm runs on three daily frustrations.

**Pain 1 — Insurer dispatch routes leads at random.** A panel firm down the road does a hack mould job for IAG; the carrier stops taking the good firm's calls for six months. No accountability. No shared certification floor.

**Pain 2 — No ANZ cert ladder that insurers or regulators actually recognise.** Anyone slaps "IICRC certified" on the van. The IICRC sits in Las Vegas with no skin in the local game. The firm cannot charge a premium because the cowboy down the street claims the same credential.

**Pain 3 — Marketing, branding, and tech burn the owner's nights.** Three agencies in five years. One website. Two ghostings after invoice two. The bookkeeper is also the website manager. The foreman is also the social-media intern at 9pm Tuesday.

Anchor the spend. A Toby-class operator — owner-operator firm with crews in vans, on 2-4 insurer panels — sits at a verified midpoint of **~AU$5,000/mo** across the categories the association replaces or augments: IICRC dues + Xactimate seats + accounting + job-management SaaS + insurance broker fees + marketing agency retainer + lead-gen platform + equipment account. One line is confirmed by signed contract: **AU$2,750/mo** to Unite-Group for the SaaS layer alone. The other seven are bottom-up estimates pending a 30-minute Toby spend audit booked for after 26 May 2026.[^1]

Five grand a month. Six suppliers. None of them coordinate.

## 3. The moat in one sentence + the bundle in 6 lines

**Member-as-a-Service is the moat.** No competitor in the 20-org competitor map[^2] offers cert + leads + marketing + software + insurance + equipment as one membership. IICRC ships cert only. RIA US ships advocacy + membership. Prime Creative ships publishing. ISSA ships an expo. AIRAH, HIA, MBA each ship trade-specific advocacy. Every single incumbent is a single-product business that the firm owner has to wrap their own operations around.

The bundle, in six lines:

1. **Certification** — Individual + Firm-level NRPG cert, AU-delivered through CARSI, IICRC-aligned (CARSI to become IICRC Approved School — partner, not fight).
2. **Leads** — Job routing from Disaster Recovery + insurer-panel referrals to verified Certified Firms.
3. **Marketing** — Done-for-You SEO, GBP, website hosting, co-branded social-content production by Synthex (the platform that built this).
4. **Software** — AI intake, scope, and floor-plan tools (RestoreAssist + DR + CARSI productised).
5. **Insurance + Advocacy** — Group rates on PI/PL through a single broker relationship; collective advocacy at the insurer scope-template table.
6. **Equipment** — Preferred-supplier pricing across CCW national distribution (Toby's account, member rates).

One membership. One invoice. One phone number.

Anchors verified inline:[^3]

- Founder AU$249/mo sits at 1.4× NADCA Associate (AU$178/mo) for an order-of-magnitude bigger bundle — *below* a single Xactimate seat (~AU$240-$280/mo).
- Firm AU$1,200/mo lands 20% below the entry tradie-agency retainer (AU$1,500/mo[^4]); the agency is the real competitor for the marketing slice, not the dues-only associations.
- Enterprise from AU$2,500 undercuts the typical full-service SMB agency ceiling (AU$5,000-$6,000/mo) while delivering insurer-panel routing no agency can offer.
- Sole-trader AU$79/mo prices 3× the IICRC Certified Firm floor — credible without competing with Firm.

The wave-0 launch copy carries that load. Section 8 below catalogues this and other open variables.

## 7. The ask on founding partners

Founding partner status carries four commitments. All four are explicit. None are buried.

1. **Founding Membership commit.** AU$249/mo, **lifetime rate-lock, cap of 100 firms total.** Price never rises while the membership is unbroken. After the 100-firm cap fills, the Founder tier closes permanently — Firm tier remains open at AU$1,200/mo.

2. **Quarterly advisory call.** 60 minutes, four times a year. Optional but valued. The agenda is the Pi-CEO board's strategic questions for the quarter — pricing tests, channel mix, advocacy priorities, the next Best Practice Brief. Founding-partner input shapes the association's direction in the first 24 months.

3. **Co-marketing rights.** One LinkedIn post per quarter, mutual amplification. The association amplifies the founding partner's firm; the founding partner amplifies the association's milestone content. No content is gated behind the founding-partner badge — both sides keep editorial control.

4. **Standards committee seat option for the first five of ten Founders.** Of the 100 Founder slots, the first ten carry an *optional* seat on the Standards Committee — the body that authors the 60-day Best Practice Briefs.[^9] Five seats reserved for the first five Founders signed. Standards-committee work is unpaid and time-bound (one half-day per quarter). The seat is a recognition mechanism, not a workload.

Nothing else. No upfront equity. No exclusivity restrictions on the founding partner's existing memberships or insurer panels. No non-compete. The founding tier is a **lifetime pricing instrument and a directional advisory relationship** — not an employment contract.
