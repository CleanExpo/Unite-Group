Duncan,

The SOW you're signing on Monday covers what we agreed: I build the Dimitri ITR Platform, you pay the milestones, your team gets a working product in ~86 days. That's Phase A. Straight-up software engineering for hire. No surprises.

This letter is about **Phase B** — what happens once the platform is live, has paying users, and has proved out the thesis. Phase B isn't binding yet and we don't need to sign anything for it now. But I want you to see where I think this is heading so when the time comes, the conversation is the second one, not the first one.

## The bigger picture

Two products, one shared engine.

**Lodgey** (or BeauHQ — your vote decides on Day 2) is the mortgage-broker product the SOW covers. ITR drafting, income verification, AFSL-compliant lender submission. Your broker network is the demand side; my build team plus the Pi-CEO swarm is the supply side. Phase A delivers this.

**ATO-APP** is something I already have in production — a forensic-accounting and audit-risk platform for accountants and SMBs they advise. Thirty-plus tables live in Supabase. MYOB connectors, FBT, CGT, jurisdictional tax engines. It's been a solo project until now.

Brokers and accountants refer to each other constantly. Every broker in your network already has an accountant on the other side of the desk. Lodgey gives us the broker channel; ATO-APP gives us the accountant channel. The cross-sell graph between them is the most valuable thing in Australian SMB software, and we'd both have a seat at it.

Underneath both sits a shared platform — myGov OAuth, SBR, ATO portal integration, the taxpayer data model. Built once, both products run on it, and any future product plugs in for free.

## The Phase B structure I'm targeting

When the platform is **live with paying users + early revenue**, here's the framework we'd negotiate around. None of this binds either of us today. The percentages are starting points, not absolutes — early traction will move them.

**A new company — Lodgey Group Pty Ltd** — would hold Lodgey as the product and an exclusive licence to operate ATO-APP. The ATO-APP code stays with Unite-Group Nexus (it predates our partnership) but the commercial upside flows through NewCo. Clean and protects both of us.

**Equity (starting point): 85/15.** Me 85%, you 15%. Ordinary shares, single class. Vested over four years with a 12-month cliff. Half the vesting is time-based; the other half is KPI-gated on broker activations, accountant signups, and revenue sourced through your network. This is real ownership, not advisory equity.

**Skin in the game:** A modest cash subscription from you at the formation of NewCo — figure ~AUD $30k — at the agreed pre-money valuation. Funds six months of platform infrastructure (Supabase scale, AFSL legal opinion, ATO certification work). Both of us have something at stake.

**Revenue share (on top of equity):** 20% of net revenue from any account directly sourced by your network, tapering over seven years (20% yrs 1-2, 15% yr 3, 10% yr 4, 5% yr 5-6, zero from yr 7). Rewards the activity of bringing customers in while the equity rewards the outcome.

**Governance:** Two board seats for me, one for you. Operating control stays with me because I'm building it and accountable for the swarm. Monthly P&L + KPI dashboard, quarterly board meeting, annual strategy.

**Exit + protection clauses** (standard, both sides):
- Founders' lock-in for 24 months from NewCo formation
- After year three, either side can trigger a buyout at independent fair-market-value with right-of-first-refusal to the other
- Drag-along / tag-along (you're protected if I exit; I'm protected if you do)
- Key-person insurance on both of us
- Change-of-control acceleration on the unvested equity (you don't get stuck if there's a sale)
- Good-leaver / bad-leaver standard clauses

**ATIA is carved out.** The Australian Trade Industry Association moat I'm building stays 100% with me. Your equity is in Lodgey Group, not in ATIA. But as a founder in Lodgey Group, you sit on the inside of the distribution network ATIA generates as it grows — members flow to products, products generate proof, proof grows membership. The compounding is what makes Phase B worth doing as partners rather than as an ongoing service contract.

## What this means for Phase A (right now)

Phase A is unchanged. You pay the milestone fees in the SOW. I build the platform. We deliver inside 86 days. Standard service engagement.

This letter creates **no current obligation on either side**. It exists so when we're 60 days post-launch and the platform has its first 50 broker users, we're having the partnership conversation from a place of shared knowledge — not negotiating from scratch under pressure.

1. **Read this letter once with your lawyer** — get a second opinion on the framework. Not for sign-off; for sanity-check.
2. **Tell me if anything in the structure feels structurally wrong** — better to know now than mid-build.

5. **Pick Lodgey or BeauHQ** when the brand-mark vote opens on Day 2.

Phase B becomes a real conversation once Dimitri is live, has users, and we have actual numbers to negotiate on. Until then, this letter sits in your file as the direction of travel.
